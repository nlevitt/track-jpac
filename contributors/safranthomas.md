# Contributor Report: Thomas Safran

Thomas Safran is the Chairman of Thomas Safran & Associates, a Los Angeles-based real estate development firm. Safran is a documented donor to several political and philanthropic organizations that support the State of Israel and Zionist advocacy, including the American Israel Public Affairs Committee (AIPAC) and Democratic Majority for Israel (DMFI).

### Documented Relationship to Israel and Zionist Advocacy

#### 1. Support for AIPAC and Related Entities
Safran’s firm maintains executive-level leadership within the American Israel Public Affairs Committee (AIPAC). Jordan Pynes, the President of Thomas Safran & Associates, serves as an Advisor on the **AIPAC Real Estate Council** and has held roles as a Vice Chair for AIPAC and an AIPAC Latino Community Liaison.
*   **Source:** [DignityMoves - Jordan Pynes Profile](https://dignitymoves.org/team/jordan-pynes/)

In political finance reporting, Safran has been identified as a significant contributor to pro-Israel causes. Investigative reports on real estate donors in the 2024 election cycle note that Safran has directed funds to pro-Israel lobbying efforts and individual candidates vetted by such organizations.
*   **Source:** [The Real Deal - Real Estate Donors Spending Big (August 2024)](https://therealdeal.com/la/2024/08/01/real-estate-donors-spend-big-in-presidential-campaign/)

#### 2. Democratic Majority for Israel (DMFI)
Safran is identified as a donor to **Democratic Majority for Israel (DMFI)**, an organization founded to maintain and strengthen support for Israel within the Democratic Party. DMFI frequently funds primary challenges against candidates deemed insufficiently supportive of Israeli policy.
*   **Source:** [Americans for Financial Reform - Wall Street Money in Washington Report (Page 65)](https://ourfinancialsecurity.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/AFR-Wall-Street-Money-in-Washington-March-2021.pdf)

#### 3. J Street Leadership
Safran is a recurring donor to **J Street**, an organization that describes itself as "pro-Israel, pro-peace." He is documented as part of the "Advocates Circle" (donors of $1,800+) in 2018 and has continued giving at higher tiers, appearing as a "Diplomats Circle" donor ($5,000+) in more recent financial disclosures.
*   **Source:** [J Street 2018 Annual Report (Page 12)](https://jstreet.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/12/2018-Annual-Report.pdf)
*   **Source:** [J Street 2024 Financial Statement (Page 9)](https://jstreet.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/J-Street-2024-Financial-Statement.pdf)

#### 4. Jewish Institutional Philanthropy
Safran holds leadership positions and provides financial support to Jewish educational and housing institutions that incorporate Zionist or pro-Israel frameworks:
*   **Menorah Housing Foundation:** Safran serves as the Vice President of this foundation, which provides affordable housing for low-income seniors within a Jewish communal context.
    *   **Source:** [UCLA Williams Institute - Thomas L. Safran Profile](https://williamsinstitute.law.ucla.edu/person/thomas-l-safran/)
*   **American Jewish University (AJU):** Safran is a documented donor to AJU (formerly the University of Judaism), an institution whose mission includes advancing Jewish civilization and supporting "Peoplehood" as a core value.
    *   **Source:** [American Jewish University Board Book (March 2019)](https://www.aju.edu/sites/default/files/2019-03/AJU%20Board%20Book%20March%202019_0.pdf)
