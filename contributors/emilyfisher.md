# Contributor Report: Emily Fisher

*Let us know if you spot an inaccuracy: trackjpac@gmail.com*

**FISHER, EMILY** (also referred to as **Emily and David Fisher**) is a prominent San Francisco-based political donor and philanthropist. She is a member of the **Fisher family**, the founders of **Gap Inc.** and the **Pisces Foundation**. Fisher is documented as a significant contributor to organizations that support the State of Israel and Zionist advocacy, often operating through family-led foundations and Bay Area Jewish communal institutions.

### 1. Institutional Affiliations and Donor Relationships
Fisher’s support for Israel and Zionist causes is primarily channeled through institutional partnerships and educational philanthropy in the California Bay Area.

*   **Wornick Jewish Day School:** Fisher and her husband, David S. Fisher, are documented as major donors to the Wornick Jewish Day School in Foster City, CA, contributing at the $100,000–$149,999 level. Emily Fisher also serves on the school's **Development Committee**. The school’s curriculum emphasizes a "deep connection to the land, people, and State of Israel" as a core pillar of its identity.
    *   *Source:* Wornick Annual Report 2024/2025 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)
*   **Jewish Community Federation and Endowment Fund (San Francisco):** The Fisher family has historically been among the largest donors to the San Francisco Federation. The Federation is a primary engine for pro-Israel funding in Northern California, allocating millions of dollars annually to Israeli national projects, including security, social services, and advocacy organizations such as the **Birthright Israel Foundation**.
    *   *Source:* [Gap Founder Donald Fisher Leaves Stamp on Community - J. Weekly](https://jweekly.com/2009/10/02/gap-founder-donald-fisher-leaves-stamp-on-community/)
*   **Koret Foundation:** Fisher is associated with the **Koret Foundation**, a major pro-Israel philanthropic organization in San Francisco. The Koret Foundation is a frequent partner with Fisher family interests in funding Zionist educational initiatives and pro-Israel advocacy in the United States and Israel.
    *   *Source:* Wornick Annual Report (Donor Lists including Koret and Fisher) (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)

### 2. Support for Zionist Advocacy and Programs
Fisher’s philanthropic activities align with "Zionist advocacy" as defined by her support for organizations that promote Jewish sovereignty and connection to Israel.

*   **Birthright Israel Foundation:** The Fisher family foundations are documented supporters of **Birthright Israel**, a program that provides free trips to Israel for young Jewish adults to strengthen their Zionist identity. David Fisher (a relative/namesake) previously served as president of the Birthright Israel Foundation, and the family’s philanthropic network remains a key financial pillar for the organization.
    *   *Source:* Birthright Israel Foundation 2024 Scroll of Honor (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)
*   **CuriOdyssey and Local Institutional Support:** Emily and David Fisher use their philanthropic profile to support Bay Area institutions (like CuriOdyssey) that often participate in joint programming or fundraising with the broader San Francisco Jewish community, further embedding their influence within pro-Israel communal circles.
    *   *Source:* CuriOdyssey Impact Report FY2021 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)

### 3. Political Advocacy and Critical Context
Critical mapping projects and activist groups have identified Emily Fisher and the broader Fisher/Gap family as "Prominent Zionist Donors" due to their role in financing the infrastructure of pro-Israel advocacy in California.

*   **Zionist Donor Identification:** Political watchdog groups identify Fisher as part of a network of "Zionist donors" who leverage their wealth from Gap Inc. and Pisces Inc. to support candidates and policies that favor the Israeli government. This includes funding for local Jewish day schools and federations that institutionalize Zionist education.
*   **Pisces Foundation and Gap Inc. Legacy:** As a third-generation member of the family, Emily Fisher's role is viewed by critics as maintaining the family's legacy of support for Israeli institutions, which began with Gap founders Donald and Doris Fisher and continued through Robert J. Fisher (founder of Pisces Foundation).
    *   *Source:* [Bob Fisher Profile - Pisces Foundation](https://piscesfoundation.org/bio/bob-fisher/)
