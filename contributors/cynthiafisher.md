# Contributor Report: FISHER, CYNTHIA

*Let us know if you spot an inaccuracy: trackjpac@gmail.com*

### **Identity and Identification Context**
Cynthia Fisher is an American healthcare entrepreneur and political donor, often identified in critical databases as a "prominent Zionist donor" due to her extensive financial contributions to organizations and political entities that support Israel. In some activist lists, such as **The Mapping Project**, she has been inaccurately identified as a founder of **Gap Inc.** and **Pisces Inc.** (source (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)). This identification likely stems from confusion with **Doris Fisher**, the actual co-founder of Gap Inc. and Pisces Inc., who is a major benefactor of Zionist organizations like the **Koret Foundation** and **Birthright Israel** (source (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)).

While not a Gap founder, Cynthia A. Fisher is a high-profile figure in her own right, serving as the founder of **ViaCord** and the advocacy group **PatientRightsAdvocate.org**, and as a director of **The Boston Beer Company** (source (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)).

### **Relationship to Zionist Advocacy and Israel**
Fisher’s support for Zionist causes and the State of Israel is documented through her involvement with pro-Israel policy groups and her substantial political giving to candidates aligned with Israeli interests.

*   **Support for Pro-Israel Legislation through ALEC:**
    Fisher is a major supporter of the **American Legislative Exchange Council (ALEC)**, an organization that actively promotes pro-Israel policies and legislation at the state level. In 2023, ALEC honored Fisher with the **Laffer Award for Economic Excellence** (source (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)). During the same period, ALEC issued model resolutions "standing in solidarity with Israel" and condemning resistance to Israeli military actions, as well as promoting anti-BDS (Boycott, Divestment, and Sanctions) legislation across various U.S. states (source (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)).

*   **Financial Support for Pro-Israel Political Candidates:**
    Fisher is a prolific donor to political candidates and PACs that prioritize U.S. military and diplomatic support for Israel. According to **OpenSecrets**, she has provided hundreds of thousands of dollars to candidates who consistently vote for unconditional military aid to Israel (source (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)).

*   **Inclusion in The Mapping Project:**
    Fisher is specifically profiled by **The Mapping Project**, an activist initiative that tracks individuals and institutions in the Massachusetts area for their "complicity in the colonization of Palestine." The project maps her as a "Zionist leader" due to her philanthropic ties and political influence used to bolster U.S.-Israel relations (source (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)).

### **Association with the Fisher (Gap Inc.) Family Foundations**
Although Cynthia Fisher is not a Gap founder, she maintains documented social and philanthropic associations with the **Fisher family of San Francisco** (the Gap founders). Both Cynthia Fisher and **Randi Fisher** (wife of Gap heir Robert J. Fisher) serve on the Board of Directors of the **National Park Foundation** (source (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)).

The Fisher family, through **Pisces Inc.** and the **Koret Foundation**, has donated tens of millions of dollars to Israeli institutions, including:
*   **Birthright Israel:** The Fisher family is a major long-term donor, funding the "Fisher Family Taglit-Birthright Israel" trips (source (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)).
*   **Koret Foundation:** The family office manages assets for the Koret Foundation, which identifies "supporting the State of Israel" as a core pillar of its mission ([source](https://koret.org/grantees/israel-at-75-the-wonder-of-a-jewish-state/)).

### **Philanthropic and Business Background**
*   **ViaCord and ViaCell:** Fisher founded ViaCord in 1993, a cord blood stem cell banking company, and later co-founded ViaCell, which went public and was sold for $300 million (source (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)).
*   **Fisher Koch Family Foundation:** Through this private foundation (co-managed with her husband, Jim Koch), Fisher directs capital toward various policy and research organizations, including those that influence U.S. foreign policy regarding the Middle East ([source](https://projects.propublica.org/nonprofits/organizations/842579341)).
