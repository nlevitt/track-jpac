# Contributor Report: Marc Nathanson

Marc Nathanson is an American entrepreneur, philanthropist, and former diplomat who has documented records of financial support and institutional involvement with Israeli entities and pro-Israel advocacy organizations.

### Relationship to Israel
Nathanson has characterized himself and his family as "longtime and strong believers and supporters of the State of Israel." This support is evidenced by significant philanthropic investments in Israeli higher education and medical research. In 2008, he and his wife established the Erika Meldola Fallek Hebrew University-UCLA Medical Student Exchange Program, which facilitates the training of Israeli medical students at UCLA [1]. Additionally, they funded an endowment at the Weizmann Institute of Science in Israel [1].

### Political and Advocacy Contributions
Nathanson's political spending has targeted candidates and initiatives that align with pro-Israel policy objectives. In 2012, he founded and contributed $100,000 to a Super PAC, the "Committee to Elect an Effective Valley Congressman," specifically to support Howard L. Berman, a legislator known for a consistently pro-Israel voting record [2]. Nathanson has also been identified as a contributor to the American Israel Public Affairs Committee (AIPAC) and the Anti-Defamation League (ADL) [3][4]. His family foundation, the Jane and Marc Nathanson Family Foundation, is a frequent donor to various Jewish and pro-Israel causes [5].

### Institutional Leadership and Awards
Nathanson has held roles within Israeli academic institutions and has received honors from organizations dedicated to Israeli state finance. 
*   **Tel Aviv University:** He served as a Visiting Fellow at Tel Aviv University’s School of Government and Policy from September 2006 to February 2007 [6].
*   **Israel Bonds:** He and his wife received the Ben Gurion Award from Israel Bonds (Development Corporation for Israel), an organization that facilitates investment in the State of Israel through the sale of bonds [7]. 

### Public Statements and Policy Activity
As Chairman of the Broadcasting Board of Governors (BBG) from 1995 to 2002, Nathanson oversaw U.S. international media during periods of regional conflict. In congressional testimony, he advocated for the use of public diplomacy to counter narratives critical of U.S. and Israeli policy in the Middle East [8]. He has maintained associations with pro-Israel figures in Los Angeles, participating in leadership discussions alongside officials from the ADL [9].
