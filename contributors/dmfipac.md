This report examines the Democratic Majority for Israel Political Action Committee (DMFI PAC), a Washington, D.C.-based organization that functions as a primary vehicle for Zionist advocacy within the Democratic Party and as a significant donor in California elections.

# Contributor Report: DMFI PAC

### 1. Organizational Overview and Mission
Founded in early 2019 by Democratic strategist Mark Mellman, the **Democratic Majority for Israel (DMFI)** and its political arm, **DMFI PAC**, were established to counter declining support for Israel among younger and progressive segments of the Democratic Party (Wikipedia (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)). The organization is currently led by President and CEO Brian Romick, a former senior advisor to Representative Steny Hoyer, and Board Chair Kathy Manning, a former U.S. Representative (DMFI Press Release (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)).

The group’s stated mission is to maintain a "proudly pro-Israel" Democratic majority in Congress and to advocate for a strong U.S.-Israel alliance (DMFI Mission (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)). DMFI PAC specifically works to elect candidates who support these goals and to defeat candidates it identifies as "anti-Israel" in primary elections (DMFI PAC About (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)).

### 2. Relationship to Zionist Advocacy
DMFI PAC’s advocacy is centered on Zionism, defined by the organization as the "modern expression of Jewish self-determination" (DMFI Homepage (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)). Key pillars of its Zionist advocacy include:
*   **Security Assistance:** The PAC lobbies for unconditional U.S. military aid and specific funding for systems like the Iron Dome (JNS (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)).
*   **Opposition to BDS:** DMFI explicitly opposes the Boycott, Divestment, and Sanctions (BDS) movement and "efforts to isolate, stigmatize or delegitimize Israel" (DMFI Guiding Principles (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)).
*   **Antisemitism Definitions:** The group successfully advocated for the Biden Administration to adopt the International Holocaust Remembrance Alliance (IHRA) definition of antisemitism, which includes certain criticisms of Israel as examples of antisemitism (DMFI Two-Year Impact Report (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)).
*   **Statehood Advocacy:** Its platform supports Israel's right to exist as a "Jewish and democratic state," often opposing candidates who suggest conditioning aid on Israeli human rights practices ([Truthout](https://truthout.org/articles/pro-israel-pacs-gear-up-for-significant-spending-to-oust-progressive-congress-members-in-2024/)).

### 3. Political Spending and Donor Base
DMFI PAC is a "hybrid PAC," allowing it to make limited direct contributions while spending unlimited amounts on independent expenditures (IE) such as television ads and mailers (Times of San Diego (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)). 
*   **Financial Scope:** In the 2022 cycle, the PAC and its affiliates reported spending over $11 million (DMFI PAC Statistics (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)). For the 2024 cycle, filings showed approximately $4.8 million to $6 million in federal spending ([JNS](https://www.jns.org/u.s.-news/campaign-spending-at-pro-israel-political-action-committees-up-in-2024)).
*   **Major Donors:** Documented contributors include billionaire **Lynn Schusterman**, Qualcomm co-founder **Andrew Viterbi**, and **Amnon Rodan**, the former chairman of California-based Rodan + Fields ([Truthout](https://truthout.org/articles/pro-israel-pac-has-spent-nearly-1-million-to-try-to-sink-nina-turners-campaign/)).

### 4. California Political Activity
While based in D.C., DMFI PAC is a prominent actor in California politics, focusing on both candidate support and party platform management.
*   **Candidate Endorsements:** The PAC has endorsed numerous California Democrats, including **Adam Schiff** (Senate), **Pete Aguilar** (House), **Ted Lieu**, **Mike Levin**, and **Jimmy Gomez** (JNS (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)). 
*   **Opposition Campaigns:** In the 2024 primary, DMFI PAC targeted candidates it deemed insufficiently pro-Israel, such as **Barbara Lee** in the Senate race and **Ammar Campa-Najjar** in the 48th District, spending over $750,000 on ads attacking the latter (Times of San Diego (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)).
*   **Platform Influence:** Within the state, DMFI organized coalitions to defeat amendments to the California Democratic Party platform that would have called for conditioning aid to Israel or endorsed the BDS movement (DMFI Two-Year Impact Report (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)).
*   **Institutional Support:** Stanford-affiliated individuals contributed over $62,000 to DMFI in the 2024 cycle, identifying it as a top destination for university-linked political giving (Stanford Daily (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)).
