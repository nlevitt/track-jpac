# Contributor Report: Gabriel, Jesse

Jesse Gabriel is a member of the California State Assembly representing the 46th District and serves as the Co-Chair of the California Legislative Jewish Caucus (CLJC). Gabriel’s political activities and legislative record are characterized by advocacy for the State of Israel and the integration of Zionist-aligned definitions of antisemitism into state policy.

### 1. Leadership of the California Legislative Jewish Caucus
Gabriel chairs the CLJC, a bipartisan group of state legislators whose mission includes strengthening "California–Israel economic, technological and cultural ties" and countering "anti-Israel narratives." 
*   **Mission and Objectives:** The caucus is documented as being "primarily dedicated to Jewish institutional interests," which includes supporting Israel in educational settings and promoting economic cooperation with the Israeli government.
    *   **Source:** [California Legislative Jewish Caucus - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/California_Legislative_Jewish_Caucus)
*   **Solidarity with Israel:** Following the events of October 7, 2023, Gabriel, on behalf of the caucus, affirmed California’s "enduring solidarity with the State of Israel" and defended the Israeli government’s right to its military response.
    *   **Source:** [Caucus Statement on Attacks Against Israel - ca.gov](https://jewishcaucus.legislature.ca.gov/news/20231007-california-legislative-jewish-caucus-releases-statement-horrific-terrorist-attacks)

### 2. Legislative Advocacy for Israel and Zionist Interests
Gabriel has authored or co-authored several measures that critics and supporters alike identify as central to Zionist advocacy within the California legislature.

*   **Ethnic Studies Curriculum Reform:** Gabriel led efforts to "pull the fire alarm" on the initial draft of California’s Ethnic Studies Model Curriculum (ESMC), successfully advocating for the removal of content supportive of the Boycott, Divestment, and Sanctions (BDS) movement and the inclusion of the International Holocaust Remembrance Alliance (IHRA) definition of antisemitism.
    *   **Source:** [Jesse Gabriel Provides Update on Ethnic Studies - Jewish Journal](https://jewishjournal.com/community/333621/jesse-gabriel-provides-update-on-ethnic-studies-curriculum-in-webinar/)
*   **AB 715 (Antisemitism in Schools):** Gabriel co-authored this legislation aimed at combating antisemitism in K-12 schools. The bill has been criticized for potentially chilling speech regarding the Israeli-Palestinian conflict and relying on the IHRA definition, which classifies certain criticisms of Israel as antisemitic.
    *   **Source:** [The fight over California's antisemitism law - CalMatters](https://calmatters.org/education/2024/10/california-antisemitism-schools-law/)
*   **AB 587 (Social Media Content Moderation):** Gabriel spearheaded this law requiring social media companies to disclose their content moderation practices regarding hate speech and extremism. Proponents, including the Anti-Defamation League (ADL), framed the bill as a tool to address "anti-Israel" and "antisemitic" content online.
    *   **Source:** [California passes bill to combat hate speech on social media - JNS](https://www.jns.org/california-legislature-passes-bill-aiming-to-combat-hate-speech-on-social-media/)
*   **Academic Boycott Opposition:** Gabriel released statements applauding the nullification of a 2024 agreement by Sonoma State University’s former president to boycott Israeli institutions, calling the academic boycott "totally unacceptable."
    *   **Source:** [Jewish Caucus Statement on Sonoma State - ca.gov](https://jewishcaucus.legislature.ca.gov/news/20240515-jewish-caucus-commends-csu-chancellor-garcia-decisive-action-rejecting-boycott-israel)

### 3. Diplomatic and Sponsored Missions to Israel
Gabriel has participated in and led high-level delegations to Israel sponsored by Zionist advocacy organizations.

*   **2024 Solidarity Mission:** In February 2024, Gabriel co-led a four-day "Solidarity Mission" to Israel and the Gaza border. The trip was funded by the Jewish Public Affairs Committee (JPAC), the Jewish Community Relations Council (JCRC), and the Jewish Federation of Greater Los Angeles.
    *   **Source:** [CA Legislators Return From Israel Trip - Constant Contact/JPAC](https://campaignlp.constantcontact.com/em/1110052303031/76d9e0f6-9538-4e41-949f-7e9b09a47301)
*   **2018 AJC Delegation:** Shortly after his election, Gabriel participated in a mission to Israel with the American Jewish Committee (AJC) Project Interchange to "foster technological innovation and boost bilateral opportunities."
    *   **Source:** [Bipartisan Delegation Visit Israel - ca.gov](https://jewishcaucus.legislature.ca.gov/news/20180724-bipartisan-delegation-california-state-legislators-visit-israel-explore-bilateral)
*   **Travel Expenditures:** Documentation indicates Gabriel has used campaign funds for travel to Israel, including approximately $1,000 for travel-related expenses in 2022.
    *   **Source:** [Jesse Gabriel used campaign funds to visit Israel - San Diego Reader](https://www.sandiegoreader.com/news/2022/aug/03/under-radar-jesse-gabriel-campaign-israel/)

### 4. Relationship to Political and Advocacy Groups
Gabriel maintains close institutional ties with the Jewish Public Affairs Committee of California (JPAC) and the Anti-Defamation League (ADL), both of which are prominent in pro-Israel advocacy. He has been endorsed by "Democrats for Israel" and recognized for his "pro-Israel and Jewish community issues" leadership.
*   **Source:** [Gabriel Endorsements and Support Statements](https://www.jessegabriel.com/support)
