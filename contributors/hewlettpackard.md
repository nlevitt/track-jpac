# Contributor Report: Hewlett-Packard (HP Inc. and Hewlett Packard Enterprise)

Hewlett-Packard (HP), following its 2015 split into **HP Inc. (HPI)** and **Hewlett Packard Enterprise (HPE)**, maintains a multi-decade relationship with the Israeli government, military, and security apparatus. Documented activities include the provision of surveillance technology for checkpoints, the maintenance of population registries used for administrative control, and extensive investment in Israel’s technology sector.

### 1. Military and Security Infrastructure
HP-branded entities have historically served as primary technology contractors for the Israel Defense Forces (IDF) and the Israeli Ministry of Defense.

*   **Biometric Checkpoint Systems:** HP’s predecessor and later HPE managed the "Basel System," an automated biometric access control system installed at military checkpoints throughout the occupied West Bank and Gaza Strip. This system uses hand scanners and facial recognition to regulate the movement of Palestinians ([AFSC Investigate](https://investigate.afsc.org/company/hp-inc); [Who Profits](https://whoprofits.org/company/hewlett-packard-enterprise-hpe/)).
*   **Naval Blockade Support:** HP provided the IT infrastructure and virtualization for the Israeli Navy, which is responsible for enforcing the blockade of the Gaza Strip ([BDS Movement](https://bdsmovement.net/news/following-major-success-our-boycotthp-campaign-we-call-escalation); [Who Profits](https://whoprofits.org/company/hewlett-packard-enterprise-hpe/)).
*   **IDF Hardware Supply:** HP Inc. has been a primary provider of personal computers and hardware to the Israeli military under contracts funded significantly through U.S. Foreign Military Financing ([AFSC Investigate](https://investigate.afsc.org/company/hp-inc)).

### 2. Administrative Control and Population Registry
HP technology facilitates the administrative systems used to categorize and monitor residents within Israel and the occupied territories.

*   **Aviv System (Population Database):** HPE provided and maintained the Itanium servers for the "Aviv System," the computerized database of Israel’s Population and Immigration Authority. This system includes the "Yesha database," which tracks Israeli citizens residing in illegal settlements in the West Bank ([Who Profits](https://whoprofits.org/report/what-is-hpes-current-involvement-in-the-israeli-occupation-industry/); [BDS Movement](https://bdsmovement.net/news/boycott-hp-campaign-update-during-gazagenocide)).
*   **Biometric Identification:** The company was contracted to produce biometric ID cards for Israeli residents, including those in settlements and East Jerusalem, which form the basis of the stratified residency system ([AFSC Investigate](https://investigate.afsc.org/company/hewlett-packard-enterprise)).

### 3. Policing and Incarceration
Documented contracts indicate ongoing support for Israeli law enforcement and prison systems.

*   **Israel Prison Service (IPS):** HPE provides servers, data storage, and maintenance for the IPS. These systems manage records for Palestinian political prisoners, including those held under administrative detention ([Who Profits](https://whoprofits.org/report/what-is-hpes-current-involvement-in-the-israeli-occupation-industry/); [BDS Movement](https://bdsmovement.net/news/following-major-success-our-boycotthp-campaign-we-call-escalation)).
*   **Israel National Police:** HPE provides and maintains data centers and IT infrastructure for the Israeli Police, including units operating within the occupied West Bank ([AFSC Investigate](https://investigate.afsc.org/company/hewlett-packard-enterprise)).

### 4. Operations in Settlements and R&D
HP and its spinoffs have maintained a physical presence and research operations in Israeli-occupied territories.

*   **Beitar Illit R&D Center:** Between 2008 and 2017, HP and subsequently HPE operated a Research and Development center in the illegal settlement of Beitar Illit in the West Bank. This facility was later transferred to DXC Technology during a subsequent corporate spinoff ([AFSC Investigate](https://investigate.afsc.org/company/hp-inc); [Who Profits](https://whoprofits.org/company/hewlett-packard-enterprise-hpe/)).
*   **Smart City Pilot:** HP implemented a "Smart City" pilot project in Ariel, an illegal settlement in the northern West Bank, providing municipal storage and wireless networking systems ([AFSC Investigate](https://investigate.afsc.org/company/hewlett-packard-enterprise)).

### 5. Strategic Investments and Acquisitions
HPE continues to expand its footprint in the Israeli tech sector through high-value acquisitions.

*   **Axis Security:** In 2023, HPE acquired the Israeli cybersecurity startup Axis Security for approximately $400 million ([Ctech/Calcalist](https://www.calcalistech.com/ctechnews/article/skmnd5jca)).
*   **Zerto:** In 2021, HPE acquired Israeli cloud data management firm Zerto for $374 million ([Poalim Equity](https://www.poalimequity.com/news/hpe-buys-israeli-cloud-data-co-zerto-for-374m)).

### 6. Institutional Response and Divestment
The company’s documented role in Israel’s security apparatus has led to widespread institutional divestment by organizations citing human rights concerns.

*   **Church Divestment:** Major denominations, including the Presbyterian Church USA, the United Church of Christ, and the Evangelical Lutheran Church in America, have voted to divest from HP-branded companies due to their role in the occupation ([BDS Movement](https://bdsmovement.net/news/following-major-success-our-boycotthp-campaign-we-call-escalation); [AFSC Investigate](https://investigate.afsc.org/company/hp-inc)).
*   **Student Resolutions:** Student governments at the University of California (Berkeley and Los Angeles) passed resolutions calling for divestment from HP, specifically citing its provision of biometric systems used at checkpoints ([AFSC Investigate](https://investigate.afsc.org/company/hp-inc)).
