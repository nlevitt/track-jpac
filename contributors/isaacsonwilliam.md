# Contributor Report: Isaacson, William

William A. Isaacson (commonly referred to as Bill Isaacson) is a Washington, D.C.-based trial lawyer and co-founder of the law firm Dunn Isaacson Rhee. He previously served as a partner at Boies Schiller Flexner and Paul, Weiss, Rifkind, Wharton & Garrison. Isaacson’s documented relationship to Israel and Zionist advocacy is primarily characterized by his legal work against organizations targeting Jewish communities and his firm’s public support for Israel.

### Involvement with the American Jewish Committee (AJC)
Isaacson is identified as a donor and lay participant in activities associated with the American Jewish Committee (AJC), a prominent pro-Israel advocacy organization.
*   **Philanthropic Support:** Isaacson and his wife, Sophia McCrocklin, are recorded as donors to institutions with Jewish communal ties, including the American University Museum where they are listed as "Circle of Friends" donors alongside Israeli diplomatic representatives.
    *   Source: [American University Museum Report](https://www.american.edu/cas/museum/upload/annual-report-2015-2016.pdf) (See page 10)
*   **Legal Recognition:** Isaacson has collaborated closely with other prominent Jewish advocates, such as Robbie Kaplan, who received the AJC’s "Learned Hand Award" for legal excellence and communal service. Isaacson served as co-counsel with Kaplan in high-profile litigation aimed at combating antisemitic movements.
    *   Source: [Lawdragon 2022 Annual Magazine via Issuu](https://issuu.com/lawdragon/docs/lawdragon-2022-annual/94) (See page 94)

### Legal Advocacy Against Antisemitic Organizations
Isaacson served as lead counsel in *Sines v. Kessler*, a landmark federal lawsuit brought against the organizers of the 2017 "Unite the Right" rally in Charlottesville, Virginia.
*   **Litigation of Antisemitic Violence:** The lawsuit targeted neo-Nazi and white supremacist organizations for conspiring to commit racially and religiously motivated violence, specifically addressing the antisemitic chants and actions of the defendants. The case resulted in a $25 million judgment against the organizers.
    *   Source: [Integrity First for America - Legal Filings](https://www.integrityfirstforamerica.org/sites/default/files/Sines%20v.%20Kessler%20-%20Complaint.pdf)
    *   Source: [The Guardian (May 2025 Report)](https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2025/may/23/pentagon-spokesperson-antisemitic-conspiracy-theory)

### Professional and Institutional Support for Israel
During his tenure as a partner at Paul, Weiss, the firm took public stances in support of Israel and against anti-Israel activism on university campuses.
*   **Joint Letter on Campus Antisemitism:** In November 2023, Paul, Weiss was among a group of major law firms that signed a joint letter to deans of elite law schools. The letter urged universities to take a "zero-tolerance" approach to antisemitism and noted that some "criticism of the state of Israel" on campuses had crossed into antisemitic harassment.
    *   Source: [Wikipedia - Paul, Weiss, Rifkind, Wharton & Garrison (Israel–Palestine conflict section)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul,_Weiss,_Rifkind,_Wharton_%26_Garrison#Israel%E2%80%93Palestine_conflict)
*   **Defense of Israel Advocacy:** Professional summaries of Isaacson’s work emphasize his role in "crisis management" for high-profile clients and his alignment with legal initiatives that defend Jewish and Israeli interests in the United States.
    *   Source: [DIR LLP Firm Profile](https://dirllp.com/william-a-isaacson/)

### Philanthropic and Political Activity
Isaacson and his firm partners are regular contributors to political causes that support a pro-Israel U.S. foreign policy.
*   **University Giving:** Isaacson is a significant donor to the University of Virginia School of Law, where his contributions support various institutional goals.
    *   Source: [UVA Law 2021-22 Annual Report via Issuu](https://issuu.com/uvalaw/docs/annualreport2021-22/100) (See page 100)
*   **Political Contributions:** Documents indicate that partners at Isaacson’s firms frequently contribute to Democratic candidates and PACs known for their pro-Israel platforms, including the Democratic Majority for Israel (DMFI).
    *   Source: [Punchbowl News (April 2026 Report)](https://punchbowl.news/readback/2026/04/18/)
