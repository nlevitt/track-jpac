# Contributor Report: Elect Jesse Gabriel for Assembly 2018

Jesse Gabriel is a member of the California State Assembly representing the 46th district (formerly the 45th) and currently serves as the Co-Chair of the [California Legislative Jewish Caucus](https://jewishcaucus.legislative.ca.gov/officers). His political committee, "Elect Jesse Gabriel for Assembly 2018" (FPPC ID No. [1402641](https://transparencyusa.org/california/candidate/jesse-gabriel)), has served as a primary vehicle for his legislative and advocacy efforts, which include a documented focus on pro-Israel policy and Zionist advocacy.

### 1. Legislative Leadership and Pro-Israel Advocacy
Gabriel has utilized his position in the California State Assembly to advance legislation specifically supportive of the State of Israel and its interests within California's jurisdiction.

*   **Recognition of Israeli-Americans:** In 2022, Gabriel introduced [House Resolution 99](https://israelusa.org/california-officially-honors-israeli-americans/), which officially honored the contributions of the Israeli-American community. The resolution was developed in coordination with the Israeli-American Civic Action Network (ICAN) and was the first of its kind to be passed by the California State Assembly.
*   **Revision of Ethnic Studies Curriculum:** Gabriel led the Jewish Caucus in a multi-year effort to revise the California [Ethnic Studies Model Curriculum](https://jewishinsider.com/2021/03/california-ethnic-studies-final-draft/). He described the initial drafts as containing "anti-Jewish" and "anti-Israel" content and successfully advocated for the removal of materials that critics characterized as overly critical of Zionism or supportive of the Boycott, Divestment, and Sanctions (BDS) movement.
*   **Nonprofit Security Grants:** He authored [Assembly Bill 1548](https://jewishcaucus.legislative.ca.gov/press-release/assemblymember-jesse-gabriel-serve-chair-california-legislative-jewish-caucus), which established the California Nonprofit Security Grant Program. While the program is open to various groups, it has been heavily utilized by Jewish institutions to mitigate risks associated with anti-Israel sentiment and antisemitism.

### 2. Delegations and International Relations
Gabriel has frequently led or participated in delegations to Israel, often funded by organizations dedicated to strengthening bilateral ties.

*   **2024 "Bearing Witness" Delegation:** In February 2024, Gabriel co-led a four-day [bipartisan delegation](https://jweekly.com/2024/03/03/state-legislators-visit-a-devastated-kibbutz-meet-with-families-of-hostages/) of California legislators to Israel. The trip was sponsored by the Jewish Community Relations Council (JCRC) Bay Area, the Jewish Federation of Los Angeles, and the Jewish Public Affairs Committee of California (JPAC). The delegation met with high-ranking officials, including [President Isaac Herzog](https://www.sacbee.com/news/politics-government/capitol-alert/article285474817.html), and visited sites affected by the October 7 attacks.
*   **AJC Project Interchange:** In 2018, Gabriel participated in a delegation sponsored by [AJC Project Interchange](https://jewishcaucus.legislative.ca.gov/press-release/bipartisan-delegation-california-state-legislators-visit-israel-explore-bilateral), an educational institute of the American Jewish Committee. The trip was designed to "strengthen Latino-Jewish, Asian-Jewish, and interfaith relations" and foster technological collaboration between California and Israel.

### 3. Institutional Relationships and Endorsements
Gabriel's political career is supported by a network of organizations that advocate for Zionism and pro-Israel policies.

*   **Organizational Ties:** Gabriel serves as a key speaker and strategist for [JPAC (Jewish Public Affairs Committee of California)](https://blogs.timesofisrael.com/attending-jpac-2024-lots-of-idealism-and-dedication/), which lobbies the state legislature on issues including support for Israel and combatting anti-Zionist movements. He has also held leadership roles with the [Jewish Federation of Greater Los Angeles](https://www.jessegabriel.com/about).
*   **Political Endorsements:** He received a "strong support" recommendation from [Democrats for Israel Los Angeles](https://www.jessegabriel.com/endorsements), which noted his "exceptional leadership on Pro-Israel and Jewish community issues."
*   **Opposition to Ceasefire Resolutions:** Following the events of October 7, 2023, Gabriel and the Jewish Caucus [issued statements](https://jewishcaucus.legislative.ca.gov/press-release/california-legislative-jewish-caucus-releases-statement-horrific-terrorist-attacks) of "enduring solidarity" with Israel. He has consistently opposed campus [divestment efforts](https://gvwire.com/2024/05/17/where-do-state-lawmakers-stand-on-war-in-gaza-campus-protests/) and municipal ceasefire resolutions, arguing that they can contribute to a climate of antisemitism.

### 4. Critical Perspectives
Gabriel's pro-Israel advocacy has drawn criticism from anti-Zionist and pro-Palestinian organizations.

*   **Allegations of Erasure:** Critics such as the [LA Progressive](https://www.laprogressive.com/israel-and-palestine/who-are-the-ca-legislative-jewish-caucus) have characterized Gabriel and the Jewish Caucus as a "pro-Israel faction" that works to "erase Palestinians from the political and educational discourse" and conflates criticism of the Israeli government with antisemitism.
*   **Indoctrination Claims:** Advocacy groups have critiqued his legislative efforts regarding the [Ethnic Studies curriculum](https://calmatters.org/education/k-12-education/2024/10/california-antisemitism-bill-ab715-newsom/), describing it as an attempt to "indoctrinate" students with pro-Israel narratives while suppressing Palestinian perspectives.
