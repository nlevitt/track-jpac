# Contributor Report: Theodore G. Fisher

### Overview
Theodore "Ted" G. Fisher is a San Francisco-based investor and a principal at Pisces Inc., the family office managing the wealth of the Fisher family, founders of Gap Inc. Fisher holds significant leadership roles in private foundations that direct substantial financial capital toward Israeli institutions and Zionist advocacy programs. His involvement is characterized by long-term board service at the Koret Foundation and multi-generational financial support for organizations facilitating Jewish-American ties to the State of Israel.

### Leadership in the Koret Foundation
Theodore G. Fisher serves as a member of the Board of Directors for the Koret Foundation, a major San Francisco-based philanthropic organization. Under the tenure of Fisher and his colleagues, the foundation has prioritized "strengthening Israel" and "combating anti-Semitism" as core pillars of its mission.

*   **Board Membership:** Fisher is officially listed as a director of the Koret Foundation.
    *   [Source: Koret Foundation Leadership](https://koret.org/about/)
*   **Israel-Specific Funding:** The Koret Foundation, under its board's direction, manages the "Koret Israel Program," which funds projects within Israel and initiatives that strengthen the U.S.-Israel relationship.
    *   [Source: Koret Foundation – Israel Programs](https://koret.org/grantees/israel-at-75-the-wonder-of-a-jewish-state/)
*   **Institutional Support for Zionist Advocacy:** The foundation provides grants to the Israel Institute, which focuses on enhancing the study of modern Israel in academic settings, often described as a means to counter anti-Zionist sentiment on campuses.

### Support for Birthright Israel and Zionist Education
Fisher and his family office, Pisces Inc., are documented supporters of Taglit-Birthright Israel, an organization that provides free trips to Israel for young Jewish adults with the explicit goal of strengthening Zionist identity and political affinity for the state.

*   **Birthright Funding:** The Fisher family, through their various entities including those managed by Theodore Fisher, is recognized as a major donor to Birthright Israel.

### Political Advocacy and Contributions
Theodore Fisher’s political activity includes contributions to candidates and committees that maintain a strong pro-Israel stance, as well as direct support for lobbying entities.

*   **AIPAC and Pro-Israel Lobbying:** Fisher has a history of contributing to the American Israel Public Affairs Committee (AIPAC) and its related political action committees, which lobby the U.S. government to maintain high levels of military and diplomatic support for Israel.
    *   [Source: FEC Disclosure - Theodore Fisher Contributions](https://www.fec.gov/data/receipts/individual-contributions/?contributor_name=Theodore+Fisher&contributor_employer=Pisces)
*   **Support for the Israel Institute:** Fisher’s board-level involvement at Koret oversees grants to the Israel Institute, an organization founded to promote "knowledge and understanding of modern Israel," which critics and proponents alike identify as a vehicle for soft-power Zionist advocacy in American higher education.

### Business and Family Context
As a principal at Pisces Inc., Fisher manages the assets derived from Gap Inc., founded by Donald and Doris Fisher. The Fisher family’s collective wealth has been a foundational pillar for Zionist philanthropy in Northern California for decades, maintaining institutional links between Silicon Valley capital and the Israeli state.
