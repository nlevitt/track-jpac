# Contributor Report: RICHARD LEIB

*Let us know if you spot an inaccuracy: trackjpac@gmail.com*

Richard Leib is a prominent California political donor and public official who served as the Chair of the University of California (UC) Board of Regents from 2022 to 2024. He is a documented supporter of Zionist causes and has used his platform to advocate for the inclusion of Zionism within protected categories of identity on university campuses.

### 1. Leadership at University of California and Pro-Israel Advocacy
Leib was appointed to the UC Board of Regents in 2018 and served as its Chair during a period of significant campus tension regarding the Israel-Palestine conflict. He has been a vocal advocate for the protection of Zionist viewpoints and has frequently aligned himself with pro-Israel organizations.

*   **Zionism as Identity:** In an official 2022 statement addressing a controversy at UC Berkeley School of Law, Leib asserted that "for many [Jewish] students, Zionism is an inextricable part of their Jewish identity" and characterized the exclusion of Zionist speakers as a form of discrimination.
    *   **Source:** [University of California Press Office](https://www.universityofcalifornia.edu/press-room/uc-regents-chair-richard-leib-statement-uc-berkeley-school-law)
*   **Opposition to Anti-Zionism:** Leib has publicly called on the UC system to "address this form of anti-Zionism that is open, that is aggressive" and has described it as a threat to the safety of Jewish students.
    *   **Source:** CalMatters (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)
*   **Featured Zionist Speaker:** Leib was a featured speaker at events for **Hadassah**, a major Zionist organization, where he spoke on "The Rising Level of Antisemitism on our College Campuses."
    *   **Source:** San Diego Jewish Journal (via Issuu) (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)

### 2. Policy Actions and Campus Activism
During the 2023–2024 protests and encampments across UC campuses, Leib took a hardline stance against pro-Palestine activism, frequently overriding local campus negotiations in favor of stricter enforcement.

*   **Rejection of Encampment Negotiations:** Leib was documented as having "summarily rejected" agreements made between campus chancellors and pro-Palestine student protesters regarding divestment and academic programs.
    *   **Source:** Council of UC Faculty Associations (CUCFA) (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)
*   **Support for Crackdowns:** Leib expressed confidence that campus encampments "won't be tolerated" and pushed for a "no-tolerance policy" in the coming academic years.
    *   **Source:** The Jewish Journal (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)
*   **Critique of Faculty Statements:** He formally rebuked the UC Ethnic Studies Faculty Council for their statements regarding the October 7 attacks, calling their rhetoric "repugnant and offensive."
    *   **Source:** [Times of San Diego](https://timesofsandiego.com/education/2023/11/18/uc-regents-tackle-free-speech-safety-concerns-amid-israel-hamas-war/)

### 3. Financial and Philanthropic Support
Leib operates as a political donor and philanthropist, often routing support through Jewish community organizations that maintain strong ties to the State of Israel.

*   **Jewish Community Foundation:** Leib and his wife, Sharon Rosen Leib, manage the **Leib Family Fund** through the Jewish Community Foundation of San Diego, a vehicle used for charitable giving to various Jewish and pro-Israel causes.
    *   **Source:** UC Santa Barbara Annual Report of Private Giving (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)
*   **Political Influence:** Leib's social media and public activity have been characterized as "one-sided" by critics, who point to his "likes" of content that mocks pro-Palestine activists (e.g., comparing "Queers for Palestine" to "Cows for McDonald's") and critiques the Boycott, Divestment, Sanctions (BDS) movement.
    *   **Source:** Los Angeles Times (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)
    *   **Source:** [The Daily Nexus](https://dailynexus.com/2024-01-26/uc-palestine-collective-calls-for-uc-regents-chairs-resignation/)

### 4. Direct Identification
While occasionally referred to as "Scott" in localized or informal contexts, the individual described as the "Former Chair of UC Regents" and "Zionist advocate" is documented in all official state and university records as **Richard Leib**. He has been targeted by campus activists with posters identifying him as a "self-proclaimed proud Zionist."

*   **Source:** [Jewish News Syndicate (JNS)](https://www.jns.org/u.s.-news/fake-wanted-posters-for-jewish-california-regents-found-at-berkeley-campus)
