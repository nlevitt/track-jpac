# Contributor Report: Monique Limón for Senate 2024

Monique Limón is the President pro tempore of the California State Senate, representing District 21 (previously District 19). Her 2024 re-election campaign and broader political career have documented ties to pro-Israel advocacy organizations and legislative actions supportive of the State of Israel.

### Documentation of Zionist Advocacy & Relationship to Israel

**1. Endorsement by Democratic Majority for Israel (DMFI)**  
Limón was formally endorsed by the Democratic Majority for Israel (DMFI) PAC for her 2024 Senate campaign. DMFI is an organization that explicitly advocates for "pro-Israel policies" within the Democratic Party and supports candidates who maintain a strong U.S.-Israel relationship.
*   **Source:** [Democratic Majority for Israel PAC – 2024 Endorsements](https://dmfi.org/endorsed-candidates/) (Note: Limón is listed under the 2024 endorsements section).
*   **Source:** [Dave Min for Congress – Endorsements List](https://davemin.com/endorsements/) (Lists Limón as a fellow DMFI-endorsed state legislator).

**2. Legislative Delegations to Israel**  
In April 2022, Limón participated in a legislative delegation to Israel during the state's spring recess. These missions, often organized by pro-Israel groups or the California Legislative Jewish Caucus, are designed to strengthen bilateral ties between California and Israel and brief lawmakers on security and economic cooperation.
*   **Source:** [San Francisco Chronicle – How Newsom and other powerful California politicians did spring break](https://www.sfchronicle.com/politics/article/How-Newsom-and-other-powerful-California-17887754.php) (Lists Monique Limón as one of the lawmakers who traveled to Israel in 2022).

**3. Support for the California Legislative Jewish Caucus Priorities**  
As a high-ranking leader in the Senate, Limón has consistently co-authored and supported resolutions and budget items prioritized by the California Legislative Jewish Caucus. This includes co-authoring Senate Concurrent Resolution 105 (SCR 105) in 2025, which addressed human rights in Gaza but was notable for its inclusion of language "condemning terrorism" and "the safe return of all hostages," a framework supported by the Jewish Caucus and other pro-Israel entities.
*   **Source:** [California State Senate – SCR 105 Co-Authors and Text](https://sd10.senate.ca.gov/news/2025-09-13-california-state-senate-becomes-first-and-only-state-legislative-body-nation-pass)

**4. Public Statements Regarding the October 7 Attacks**  
Immediately following the events of October 7, 2023, Limón issued a public statement expressing "devastation" over the "horrific attack against civilians in Israel," framing the conflict initially around the security of Israeli civilians.
*   **Source:** [The Santa Barbara Independent – Peace Is on the Ballot](https://www.independent.com/2024/02/06/peace-is-on-the-ballot/)

**5. Funding for Pro-Israel Interest Facilities**  
During her tenure as a leader in the Senate, the state budget included significant earmarks for security at Jewish private schools and community centers, including a $2.5 million allocation for security infrastructure at Milken Community School, which she supported as part of the broader leadership budget framework.
*   **Source:** [CalMatters – California lawmakers found money for these pet projects](https://calmatters.org/politics/2025/11/california-budget-earmarks-prop-4/) (Mentions Limón's role as the incoming Senate leader during the allocation of these funds).
