# Contributor Report: Rich Leib

Richard "Rich" Leib is a California-based political donor, strategic consultant, and member of the University of California (UC) Board of Regents. He served as the Board’s Chair from 2022 to 2024 and has been a prominent advocate for Israeli interests and Zionist causes within the state’s public university system. Leib has publicly identified as a Zionist and has frequently leveraged his position to condemn anti-Zionist activism on UC campuses.

### 1. Institutional Role and Zionist Advocacy
Leib has utilized his platform as Chair of the UC Regents to advocate for policies and statements supportive of the State of Israel. He has explicitly defined Zionism as the belief in the right of Israel to exist and has characterized efforts to exclude Zionist viewpoints as inconsistent with the university’s mission.

*   **Self-Identification:** Leib has publicly stated, "I think all these things have been inflamed... probably most people in this room right here are Zionists if they believe in just the existence of the state of Israel, which is all it is."
    *   [Source: UC Regent Meeting Transcript/YouTube (Nov 17, 2022)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ggv1nzrwpMA)
*   **Opposition to Academic Boycotts:** In October 2022, Leib issued a formal statement denouncing a bylaw adopted by student organizations at UC Berkeley School of Law that prohibited inviting speakers who support Zionism or the "apartheid state of Israel." Leib called the move "deplorable" and "anti-Semitic."
    *   [Source: University of California Press Room (Oct 13, 2022)](https://www.universityofcalifornia.edu/press-room/uc-regents-chair-richard-leib-statement-uc-berkeley-school-law)
    *   Source: AAC&U Report on Campus Anti-Zionism (Mar 20, 2023) (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)

### 2. Response to October 7 and Conflict in Gaza
Following the October 7, 2023, Hamas-led attack on Israel, Leib issued joint statements with UC President Michael Drake condemning the event as "terrorism" and an "act of unspeakable terror." He subsequently engaged in public disputes with UC faculty groups who questioned the administration's framing of the conflict.

*   **Condemnation of Faculty Stance:** Leib described a letter from the UC Ethnic Studies Faculty Council—which requested the university retract its use of the word "terrorism" and stand against "Israel’s war crimes"—as "repugnant and offensive."
    *   Source: CalMatters (Nov 15, 2023) (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)
    *   Source: Los Angeles Times (Oct 31, 2023) (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)
*   **Critique of "Anti-Zionism":** During a November 2023 Regents meeting, Leib urged the UC system to address "anti-Zionism that is open, that is aggressive, and that is attacking every one of our students."
    *   [Source: San Diego Jewish World (Nov 15, 2023)](https://www.sdjewishworld.com/2023/11/15/uc-board-of-regents-chair-rich-leib-incumbent-on-university-leadership-to-call-out-hate-speech/)

### 3. Documentation of Controversial Advocacy and Social Media Activity
Leib has faced significant criticism from pro-Palestinian organizations for his social media engagement, which critics argue demonstrates a "one-sided" bias that dehumanizes Palestinians and justifies Israeli military actions.

*   **Resignation Demands:** In late 2023, the UC Palestine Collective, representing over 115 faculty, student, and community groups, called for Leib’s resignation. The group cited Leib’s "likes" on X (formerly Twitter) of posts that mocked pro-Palestinian protesters and endorsed claims that Hamas used hospitals as military bases.

    *   [Source: The Daily Nexus (Jan 26, 2024)](https://dailynexus.com/2024-01-26/uc-palestine-collective-calls-for-uc-regents-chairs-resignation/)
*   **Specific Allegations:** Documentation provided by critics included screenshots of Leib "liking" a post that compared "Queers for Palestine" to "Cows for McDonald's"—a comparison labeled by advocates as "pinkwashing" intended to justify occupation.

### 4. Political Background and Donor Activity
Leib’s professional career includes serving as staff for prominent pro-Israel politicians, and his financial contributions align with establishment Democratic causes often associated with pro-Israel advocacy.

*   **Political Career:** Leib worked on the staff of U.S. Congressmen Henry Waxman and Mel Levine, both of whom were known as key leaders of the pro-Israel lobby in Congress during their tenures.
