# Contributor Report: Ron Fisher

*Let us know if you spot an inaccuracy: trackjpac@gmail.com*

Ron Fisher (Ronald D. Fisher) is a prominent technology executive and philanthropist documented as a significant donor to Zionist organizations and Israeli educational initiatives. He is most frequently identified in philanthropic records alongside his wife, **Lisa Rosenbaum**, through the **Fisher Family Foundation**. His advocacy is characterized by substantial financial support for programs designed to strengthen the connection between the Jewish diaspora and the State of Israel.

### 1. Philanthropic Support for Israel and Zionist Initiatives

Ron Fisher’s documented philanthropy focuses on organizations that promote Jewish identity and provide educational and immersive experiences in Israel.

*   **Birthright Israel Foundation:** Fisher is a documented supporter of the Birthright Israel Foundation, which provides free 10-day trips to Israel for young Jewish adults to "transform the Jewish future" and cement their relationship with the country. He has been listed in foundation records as a donor contributing to the organization's mission of connecting young Jews to their heritage and homeland.

*   **AMIT Children:** Fisher and Lisa Rosenbaum are members of AMIT's "Founders' Circle," a designation for donors with a cumulative giving history of $100,000 or more. AMIT is a major Zionist organization that operates a network of schools in Israel focused on Jewish values-based education and "ensuring that Israel will remain a strong and self-reliant nation." In November 2023, Fisher was noted for his involvement in "Solidarity Missions" to Israel to support faculty and students following the October 7 attacks.

*   **MISTI MIT-Israel Program:** Through the Fisher Family Foundation, Fisher and Rosenbaum are major benefactors of the MIT-Israel program. This initiative facilitates research internships and professional collaborations between MIT students and Israeli technology and academic centers, such as the **Technion** and **Tel Aviv University**, to foster "dynamic connections" between the U.S. and Israeli innovation ecosystems.

### 2. Professional and Economic Advocacy

Ron Fisher’s professional role as a Senior Advisor and former Vice Chairman of **SoftBank Group Corp.** has coincided with massive capital investment into the Israeli technology sector. Under his leadership and advisory, SoftBank’s **Vision Fund** has directed billions of dollars into Israeli startups, an activity often framed as critical to the "Startup Nation" narrative and the economic stability of the State of Israel.

*   **SoftBank Israel Investments:** SoftBank has invested in major Israeli firms such as **AnyVision**, **Cato Networks**, and **Trax**. These investments represent a significant bridge between global finance and the Israeli economy.

    *   **Source:** [SoftBank Vision Fund Portfolio](https://visionfund.com/portfolio)

### 3. Institutional Affiliations and Donor Networks

Fisher is often associated with the **Koret Foundation**, another major San Francisco-based funder of Zionist causes. While Fisher’s own foundation operates independently, the two entities frequently co-fund the same Israeli educational and advocacy programs (e.g., MIT-Israel and Birthright).
