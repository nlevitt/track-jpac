# Contributor Report: Mark Leno

Mark Leno is a former California State Senator (2008–2016) and Assemblymember (2002–2008). His political career and personal background reflect a consistent and documented affiliation with Zionist organizations and pro-Israel legislative advocacy.

### 1. Affiliation with Zionist Organizations
Leno’s relationship with Zionist advocacy is established through his leadership and membership in several prominent organizations:

*   **California Legislative Jewish Caucus (CLJC):** Leno was a founding member of the CLJC in 2012. The caucus is dedicated to "supporting Israel in schools and college campuses," "countering anti-Zionism and anti-Israel narratives," and "strengthening California–Israel economic, technological and cultural ties."
    *   **Source:** [Wikipedia: California Legislative Jewish Caucus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/California_Legislative_Jewish_Caucus)
    *   **Source:** [J. Weekly: Capitol clout: Jewish legislators form strategic caucus in Sacramento](https://jweekly.com/2014/02/07/capitol-clout-jewish-legislators-form-strategic-caucus-in-sacramento/)
*   **Jewish Community Relations Council (JCRC):** Leno has a long-term relationship with the JCRC Bay Area, an organization that advocates for the State of Israel. He has been identified as leading a California legislative delegation to Israel organized by the JCRC.
    *   **Source:** [J. Weekly: Mark Leno -- from rabbinical student to supe](https://jweekly.com/1998/04/10/mark-leno-from-rabbinical-student-to-supe/)
*   **A Wider Bridge:** Leno is a documented supporter and signatory for "A Wider Bridge," a pro-Israel organization that builds connections between North American LGBTQ communities and Israel. He signed a 2017 public letter organized by the group to advocate within the Israeli political system.
    *   **Source:** [J. Weekly: Bay Area Jews condemn same-sex adoption inequality in Israel](https://jweekly.com/2017/07/26/bay-area-jews-condemn-same-sex-adoption-inequality-israel/)
    *   **Source:** [A Wider Bridge: 2022 Annual Report (Contributor List)](https://awiderbridge.org/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/AWB_AnnualReport_2022_v5-2.pdf)

### 2. Legislative Advocacy for Israel
During his tenure in the California State Legislature, Leno supported measures designed to oppose the Boycott, Divestment, and Sanctions (BDS) movement and strengthen ties with Israel:

*   **Opposition to UC Campus Divestment:** In June 2013, Leno was one of 36 state legislators who signed a letter to the University of California Regents congratulating them for their stance against student government resolutions urging divestment from companies doing business with Israel. The letter characterized the BDS movement as "divisive and harmful."
    *   **Source:** [JPAC: State Legislators Oppose Divestment on UC campuses](https://jpac-cal.org/2013/07/03/state-legislators-oppose-divestment-on-uc-campuses/)
*   **Support for California-Israel Memorandum of Understanding (MOU):** As a key member of the Legislative Jewish Caucus, Leno supported SCR 121 (2014), which formally endorsed a memorandum of understanding between California and Israel to foster economic and technological cooperation.
    *   **Source:** [Wikipedia: California Legislative Jewish Caucus (Origins and early Israel economic mandate)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/California_Legislative_Jewish_Caucus)

### 3. Personal and Educational Ties to Israel
Leno’s relationship with Israel is also rooted in his education and religious background:

*   **Education in Jerusalem:** Leno was the valedictorian of his graduating class at the American College of Jerusalem (now Jerusalem University College), where he earned his Bachelor of Arts degree.
    *   **Source:** [California State Senate Archive: Mark Leno Biography](https://archive.senate.ca.gov/senators/leno)
*   **Zionist Statements:** In response to local boycotts of Israeli products, Leno stated, "The Jewish voice is alive and well and will not be silenced. We can never ever let our voices be silenced." In a 2002 interview, he reaffirmed his position: "Do I support the right of Israel to exist? Of course. Without question."
    *   **Source:** [J. Weekly: Jews return to Rainbows aisles but maintain watchful eye](https://jweekly.com/2003/01/03/jews-return-to-rainbows-aisles-but-maintain-watchful-eye/)
    *   **Source:** [J. Weekly: S.F. runoff heats up over candidates' Mideast views](https://jweekly.com/2002/11/22/s-f-runoff-heats-up-over-candidates-mideast-views/)
