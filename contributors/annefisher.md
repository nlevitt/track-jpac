# Contributor Report: FISHER, ANNE

## Overview
**Anne B. Fisher** is a prominent California-based political donor and philanthropist, primarily recognized through her marriage to **Robert J. Fisher**, the son of **Gap Inc.** founders Donald and Doris Fisher. The Fisher family, through their family office **Pisces Inc.** and various charitable foundations, are significant contributors to San Francisco’s Jewish communal infrastructure and various Zionist advocacy organizations. Their philanthropic footprint includes substantial support for the **Koret Foundation**, **Birthright Israel**, and the **American Israel Public Affairs Committee (AIPAC)**.

## Relationship to Gap Inc. and Pisces Inc.
Anne B. Fisher is the wife of Robert J. Fisher, who has served as the Chairman of the Board and interim CEO of Gap Inc. [1.18](https://s204.q4cdn.com/320226404/files/doc_financials/2026/ar/Gap-Inc-2026-Proxy-Statement.pdf). The family's wealth and political influence are managed through **Pisces Inc.**, an investment group and family office where Robert Fisher serves as Managing Director [1.14](https://piscesfoundation.org/bio/bob-fisher/). ## Documented Support for Israel and Zionist Advocacy

### 1. AIPAC and Political Lobbying
The Fisher family has a documented history of supporting **AIPAC**, the leading pro-Israel lobbying group in the United States. Federal Election Commission (FEC) records show that individuals associated with Pisces Inc. have made significant contributions to the **American Israel Public Affairs Committee Political Action Committee** 1.13 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation). Donald and Doris Fisher, the Gap founders, were identified as part of AIPAC’s high-level donor network, a tradition continued within the family’s philanthropic circle 1.5 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation).

### 2. Birthright Israel
The Fisher family is recognized as a stakeholder and major donor to the **Birthright Israel Foundation**, an organization that funds free 10-day trips to Israel for young Jewish adults to strengthen their connection to the state [1.3](https://mmfisher.org/grant-partner/birthright-israel-foundation/). While another branch of the Fisher family (the Nine West founders Jerome and Anne Fisher) is noted for a $50 million gift, the San Francisco Fishers (Gap/Pisces) are also consistent supporters of the foundation's mission [1.3](https://www.jta.org/2009/02/04/united-states/sixteen-jews-among-chronicle-of-philanthropys-top-50-donors).

### 3. Koret Foundation
Anne and Robert Fisher are deeply integrated into the San Francisco Jewish philanthropic network that funds the **Koret Foundation**. The Koret Foundation is one of the largest pro-Israel grantmakers in the Bay Area, focusing on Jewish identity and support for the State of Israel [1.6](https://jweekly.com/2009/10/02/gap-founder-donald-fisher-leaves-stamp-on-community/). The Fishers are major supporters of the **Jewish Community Federation and Endowment Fund**, which works closely with Koret to direct funding toward Zionist causes and Israeli infrastructure [1.6](https://jweekly.com/2009/10/02/gap-founder-donald-fisher-leaves-stamp-on-community/).

### 4. Community Institutional Support
The family’s support for Zionist identity is visible in the naming of the **Fisher Family Hall** at the **Jewish Community Center of San Francisco (JCCSF)**, an institution that serves as a hub for pro-Israel programming and Jewish cultural advocacy in California [1.6](https://jweekly.com/2009/10/02/gap-founder-donald-fisher-leaves-stamp-on-community/).
