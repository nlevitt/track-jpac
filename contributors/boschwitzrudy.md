# Contributor Report: Boschwitz, Rudy

### Overview
Rudy Boschwitz is a former United States Senator from Minnesota (1979–1991) and a former U.S. Ambassador to the United Nations Commission on Human Rights. His political and professional career has been defined by long-standing advocacy for the State of Israel, ranging from legislative efforts to increase foreign aid to executive leadership in organizations within the pro-Israel lobby.

### Legislative and Policy Advocacy
During his tenure in the U.S. Senate, Boschwitz utilized his committee assignments to prioritize the U.S.-Israel strategic relationship.
*   **Foreign Aid and Strategic Alliance:** As chairman of the Senate Foreign Relations Subcommittee on Near Eastern and South Asian Affairs, Boschwitz was a primary architect of expanded foreign aid to Israel. He argued that aid to Israel provided a greater return on investment for U.S. interests than spending on NATO ([Freedom Foundation](https://freedomfoundationofminnesota.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Legacy_of_Leadership_Report.pdf)).
*   **Ethiopian Jewish Caucus:** Boschwitz founded and led a caucus in the Senate focused on the emigration of Ethiopian Jews, which eventually included approximately 40 members ([TC Jewfolk](https://tcjewfolk.com/rudy-boschwitz-revisits-role-in-operation-solomon/)).
*   **Opposition to Arab Arms Sales:** In the 1980s, Boschwitz was a vocal opponent of arms deals to Arab nations that were perceived as threats to Israeli security, including the proposed sale of Maverick missiles to Saudi Arabia ([UT Austin / The Power Game](https://la.utexas.edu/users/chenry/egv/2007/04/the-power-game/)).

### Diplomatic and Organizational Leadership
Following his Senate career, Boschwitz continued his advocacy through diplomatic appointments and board memberships in Zionist organizations.
*   **Operation Solomon:** In 1991, acting as an emissary for President George H.W. Bush, Boschwitz led the negotiations with the Ethiopian government that resulted in Operation Solomon. This mission involved the military airlift of over 14,000 Ethiopian Jews to Israel ([Jewish Virtual Library](https://www.jewishvirtuallibrary.org/rudolph-quot-rudy-quot-boschwitz)).
*   **Board Memberships:** Boschwitz serves on the Board of Directors of the **American Israel Public Affairs Committee (AIPAC)** and the **Jewish Institute for National Security of America (JINSA)**, two of the most prominent organizations advocating for pro-Israel policies in Washington ([Hudson Institute](https://www.hudson.org/experts/398-rudy-boschwitz)).
*   **U.N. Commission on Human Rights:** As U.S. Ambassador to the commission in 2005, Boschwitz frequently countered international criticism of Israeli military and settlement policies, characterizing U.N. resolutions against Israel as biased ([YouTube / JBS Interview](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FqS0-u2Y0x4)).

### Political Funding and Alignment
Boschwitz’s political identity was closely tied to his status as a "pro-Israel" candidate, both as a recipient of funds and a fundraiser.
*   **PAC Funding:** During his 1990 re-election campaign, Boschwitz received $71,829 from pro-Israel Political Action Committees (PACs), while his opponent received none ([Jewish Telegraphic Agency](https://www.jta.org/1990/11/01/archive/the-1990-elections-pro-israel-pacs-providing-funds-to-candidates)). Cumulative data through 2024 identifies him as having received over $244,000 in pro-Israel contributions throughout his career ([Factifada / OpenSecrets](https://nigelparry.org/factifada/recipients.html)).
*   **The "Jewish Letter":** In 1990, the Boschwitz campaign issued a controversial "Jewish Letter" to Minnesota voters. The document questioned the Jewish credentials of his opponent, Paul Wellstone, citing Wellstone's marriage to a non-Jew and his support for Palestinian representation as evidence that he was not a "supporter of Israel" ([TC Jewfolk](https://tcjewfolk.com/the-jewish-letter-20-years-later/)).
*   **Fundraising for Pro-Israel Candidates:** Boschwitz has been identified as a key fundraiser for Republican candidates who align with his views on Israel, including serving as a "Bush Pioneer" and "Bush Ranger" ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rudy_Boschwitz)).

### Public Positions
Boschwitz has maintained a consistent public stance against a two-state solution and in favor of continued Israeli control over disputed territories.
*   **Skepticism of Two-State Solution:** In interviews, he has expressed that a two-state solution is "almost impossible" due to what he describes as the educational indoctrination of Palestinian children toward "martyrdom" ([YouTube / JBS Interview](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FqS0-u2Y0x4)).
*   **Defense of Settlements:** He has argued that Palestinians living in the West Bank enjoy greater freedoms under Israeli administration than they would under Palestinian governance ([YouTube / JBS Interview](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FqS0-u2Y0x4)).
