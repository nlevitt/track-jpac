# California Contributor Report: Kotick, Robert

This report documents the political contributions and advocacy activities of Robert "Bobby" Kotick, a California-based businessman and former CEO of Activision Blizzard, with a specific focus on his support for Israel and Zionist advocacy.

### 1. Public Advocacy and Open Letters
Following the October 7, 2023, attacks on Israel, Kotick participated in high-profile advocacy efforts to support the Israeli state and condemn the actions of Hamas.

*   **Creative Community for Peace (CCFP) Open Letter:** Kotick was among more than 2,000 entertainment industry leaders who signed an open letter released by the non-profit organization Creative Community for Peace. The letter expressed support for Israel, condemned Hamas, and urged the entertainment community to refrain from sharing "misinformation" about the conflict.

### 2. Organizational Involvement and Investments
Kotick's support for Israel is further evidenced by his financial investments in media and his participation in major Jewish communal leadership forums.

*   **Investment in The Free Press:** Kotick is a documented investor in *The Free Press* (formerly *Common Sense*), a media outlet founded by Bari Weiss. The publication has been described by commentators and media reports as having a "pro-Israel" editorial stance and has published extensive content condemning anti-Zionism.
    *   **Source:** Wikipedia - The Free Press (Investment details) (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)
*   **Conference of Presidents of Major American Jewish Organizations:** In June 2023, Kotick was identified as a participant in a convening held by the Conference of Presidents of Major American Jewish Organizations in New York. The gathering focused on the White House's national strategy to combat antisemitism and included briefings from high-level administration officials.
    *   **Source:** Jewish Insider - Daily Kickoff (June 1, 2023) (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)
*   **USC Shoah Foundation:** Kotick and his former wife, Nina, are listed as donors to the USC Shoah Foundation, an organization founded by Steven Spielberg that preserves the testimonies of Holocaust survivors and is closely linked to Jewish and Israeli educational initiatives.

### 3. Political Contributions and LLC Activity
In California and at the federal level, Kotick has utilized personal funds and corporate entities to influence political outcomes.

*   **California Proposition 50 (2025):** Kotick contributed $990,000 to the committee opposing California Proposition 50, a ballot measure related to congressional redistricting. This made him one of the largest individual donors in the campaign.
    *   **Source:** [Ballotpedia - California Proposition 50](https://ballotpedia.org/California_Proposition_50,_Authorize_New_Congressional_Districts_Map_Measure_(2025))
    *   **Source:** LAist - Donors raise nearly $130M in the battle over California redistricting (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)
*   **Use of LLCs for Political Giving:** Investigations into Kotick’s political spending revealed the use of LLCs, such as **Norgate LLC** and **807080A LLC**, to facilitate contributions. Norgate LLC reportedly contributed approximately $500,000 to the Senate Leadership Fund, while 807080A LLC contributed to various GOP primary candidates. Both entities shared addresses with Kotick-connected foundations.
    *   **Source:** [Shacknews - Bobby Kotick's secret companies for political campaigns](https://www.shacknews.com/article/128864/nintendo-wii-u-and-3ds-eshops-will-close-permanently-in-march-2023)
*   **California State Candidates:** Kotick has made direct contributions to California state-level candidates, including a $2,000 donation to Dave Cortese's campaign.
    *   **Source:** [Transparency USA - Dave Cortese Contributor List](https://www.transparencyusa.org/ca/candidate/dave-cortese-can)
