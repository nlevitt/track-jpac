# California Contributor Report: Glazer, Steven

Steven Mitchell Glazer is a former California State Senator who represented District 7 from 2015 to 2024. Throughout his tenure, Glazer established a record of legislative support for the State of Israel and was a prominent member of the California Legislative Jewish Caucus, a body that advocates for policies aligned with Zionist interests.

### Documented Relationship to Israel and Zionist Advocacy

#### Opposition to Boycott, Divestment, and Sanctions (BDS)
Glazer has consistently opposed the Boycott, Divestment, and Sanctions (BDS) movement. In 2017, he was part of a group of California legislators who documented their opposition to divestment from Israel in a letter addressed to the state’s primary pension funds, CalPERS and CalSTRS. The letter urged the funds to maintain their investments in Israel despite calls from activists for divestment due to human rights concerns.

*   **2017 Divestment Opposition:** Glazer was identified as a signatory to a 2017 letter urging California pension funds not to divest from Israeli assets in response to BDS campaigning. This alignment followed the passage of AB 2844 (2016), a bill supported by the Jewish Caucus that effectively barred the state from contracting with companies participating in a boycott of Israel.

#### Legislative Support for Zionist Advocacy
Glazer authored and supported several key pieces of legislation aimed at protecting Israeli interests and curbing pro-Palestinian activism on California campuses.

*   **SB 1287 (2024):** Glazer introduced Senate Bill 1287, which required California’s public colleges and universities to adopt and expand student conduct codes to prohibit "calls for genocide." The bill was specifically crafted in the context of the 2023–2024 Israel-Gaza War protests. Critics argued the bill’s language was designed to suppress pro-Palestinian speech and demonstrations on campus.

*   **Funding for Zionist Institutions:** As a member of the Jewish Caucus, Glazer successfully advocated for millions of dollars in state funding for the Museum of Tolerance (Simon Wiesenthal Center) and other institutions that promote Zionist narratives and provide training to educators on "anti-Israel discrimination."

#### Public Statements and Diplomatic Engagement
Glazer has utilized his political platform to affirm his support for Israel through official visits and public messaging.

*   **2021 Israel Visit:** In 2021, Glazer participated in a legislative trip to Israel where he visited Kibbutz Kfar Aza. Following the October 7, 2023, attacks, he frequently referenced this visit to advocate for Israeli military action and state support.

*   **Condemnation of Anti-Zionist Sentiment:** Glazer has been a vocal critic of pro-Palestinian slogans used in California. He has supported efforts to define certain phrases, such as "From the river to the sea," as antisemitic and has pushed for university leadership to take disciplinary action against protesters.
