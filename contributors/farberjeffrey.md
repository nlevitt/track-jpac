# Contributor Report: Jeffrey Farber

### Overview
Jeffrey Farber is the Chief Executive Officer of the [Koret Foundation](https://koret.org/about/), a San Francisco-based private foundation with assets reported at approximately [$473 million](https://medium.com/@Countertrac/san-franciscos-koret-foundation-funds-pro-settler-and-anti-muslim-hate-groups-684e27f4f693) in 2014 and [$316 million](https://projects.propublica.org/nonprofits/organizations/941624987) in 2024. Farber has held this leadership position since 2005. Under his direction, the foundation has prioritized "U.S.-Israel Bridge Building" and "Jewish Peoplehood" as core pillars of its grantmaking strategy, focusing on strengthening the strategic alliance between the United States and Israel.

### Leadership in Zionist Advocacy Organizations
Beyond his role at the Koret Foundation, Farber has held senior leadership positions in major pro-Israel advocacy organizations:
*   **AIPAC (American Israel Public Affairs Committee):** Farber is the [past Chair of AIPAC of Northern California](https://koret.org/insights/israel-at-75-the-wonder-of-a-jewish-state/) and has served on the [AIPAC National Executive Committee](https://medium.com/@Countertrac/san-franciscos-koret-foundation-funds-pro-settler-and-anti-muslim-hate-groups-684e27f4f693). AIPAC is a primary lobbying group in the U.S. dedicated to advancing pro-Israel policies in Congress.
*   **A Wider Bridge:** In 2022, Farber was awarded the [Tzedek (Righteousness) Award](https://koret.org/news-archives/we-are-honored-to-celebrate-jeff-farber/) by A Wider Bridge, an organization that builds support for Israel within the LGBTQ community and [combats anti-Zionism](https://awiderbridge.org/new-york-gala-2023/). He and his wife, Barbara Farber, are documented as [donors to the organization](https://awiderbridge.org/annual-report-2022/).

### Institutional Funding of Israeli Entities
Under Farber’s leadership, the Koret Foundation has directed significant capital toward Israeli academic and humanitarian institutions:
*   **Tel Aviv University (TAU):** In 2022, the foundation announced a [$10 million grant](https://koret.org/news-archives/10-million-koret-foundation-grant-to-establish-the-koret-center-for-jewish-civilization-at-tel-aviv-university-in-partnership-with-anu-museum-of-the-jewish-people/) to establish the Koret Center for Jewish Civilization at TAU. This followed a previous [$10 million grant in 2018](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Koret_Foundation) for bioinformatics and smart cities collaborations.
*   **Shalem College:** The foundation provides ongoing support to Shalem College, including a [$5 million grant](https://koret.org/news-archives/building-bridges-between-bay-area-and-israeli-jews/) for the Koret Jewish Peoplehood Project, which aims to educate students on the "pro-Israel movement in the US" and the "relationship between Israel and diaspora Jewry."
*   **IsraAID:** Koret partnered with [IsraAID](https://koret.org/grantee-spotlight/israaid-bridge-building-with-israel-through-global-humanitarian-aid/) to establish its U.S. headquarters, promoting the organization as a means to "strengthen the U.S.-Israel relationship through volunteerism."

### Support for Pro-Israel Advocacy & Military Organizations
The Koret Foundation has been documented as a financier for various organizations that advocate for Israeli interests and support its military:
*   **American Israel Education Foundation (AIEF):** The foundation has [funded AIEF](https://medium.com/@Countertrac/san-franciscos-koret-foundation-funds-pro-settler-and-anti-muslim-hate-groups-684e27f4f693), the charitable affiliate of AIPAC, which conducts educational seminars for members of Congress.
*   **Friends of the Israel Defense Forces (FIDF):** Documented grants have been provided to [FIDF](https://medium.com/@Countertrac/san-franciscos-koret-foundation-funds-pro-settler-and-anti-muslim-hate-groups-684e27f4f693), which raises funds to provide [resources and well-being support](https://www.fidf.org/foundations/) to Israeli soldiers.
*   **StandWithUs:** The foundation has provided financial support to [StandWithUs](https://medium.com/@Countertrac/san-franciscos-koret-foundation-funds-pro-settler-and-anti-muslim-hate-groups-684e27f4f693), an organization that focuses on pro-Israel campus advocacy and has been criticized by Palestinian rights activists for attempting to [stifle debate](https://medium.com/@Countertrac/san-franciscos-koret-foundation-funds-pro-settler-and-anti-muslim-hate-groups-684e27f4f693) on Palestinian rights.
*   **Israel on Campus Coalition (ICC):** The foundation [funds the ICC](https://koret.org/), which is dedicated to "inspiring college students in the U.S. to see Israel as a source of pride" and training them as advocates.

### Critical Allegations and Documented Impact
Farber’s tenure has seen the foundation criticized for its funding of "right-wing" and "pro-settler" groups. Reports have highlighted Koret’s financial ties to the [Center for Security Policy, the Middle East Forum, and Investigative Project on Terrorism](https://medium.com/@Countertrac/san-franciscos-koret-foundation-funds-pro-settler-and-anti-muslim-hate-groups-684e27f4f693), organizations that have been labeled as part of an "Islamophobia Network." 

Farber has publicly maintained that his commitment is to [ensure a strong U.S.-Israel relationship](https://koret.org/insights/israel-at-75-the-wonder-of-a-jewish-state/), stating that he remains "amazed" by the country's strength in protecting the "homeland for the Jewish people." Following the October 7 attacks, the foundation [mobilized additional support](https://koret.org/news-archives/united-for-the-jewish-state-koret-grantees-supporting-israel-and-jews-worldwide/) for groups like JCRC (Jewish Community Relations Council) Bay Area and the Shalom Hartman Institute to advocate for U.S. support of Israel.
