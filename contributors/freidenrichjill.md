# Contributor Report: Jill Freidenrich

Jill Freidenrich is a California-based philanthropist and political donor who serves in leadership capacities for several organizations that focus on strengthening ties between the United States and Israel. Her documentation primarily links her to Zionist advocacy through large-scale financial contributions to Israeli emergency funds and governance roles in foundations that prioritize Jewish "peoplehood" and Israeli institutional support.

### Documented Zionist Advocacy and Support for Israel

#### Philanthropic Contributions to Israel-Focused Funds
Freidenrich is a documented donor to the **Jewish Federation of the Bay Area**, specifically contributing to the **Israel Emergency Fund** established following the events of October 7, 2023. According to the Federation’s 2024 Annual Report, she (listed with her late husband, John Freidenrich) donated between **$50,000 and $99,999** to this fund, which provides direct aid, medical care, and specialized services within Israel [1.1]. The Federation's mission explicitly includes "strengthening Jewish identity" through "Israel travel programs" and funding for "Northern California Hillels" in response to campus activism [1.1].

#### Leadership in Pro-Israel Philanthropic Institutions
Freidenrich has held governance roles in organizations that integrate Israeli advocacy into their core mission:
*   **Taube-Koret Campus for Jewish Life:** Freidenrich served on the board of this Palo Alto-based institution [1.2, 1.3]. The campus was established with significant funding from Tad Taube and the Koret Foundation, entities that explicitly support the "survival of Jewish life and culture... especially in Israel" [1.4].
*   **Jewish Community Endowment Fund:** She served as a board member of this fund, which manages capital directed toward Jewish causes, including significant allocations for Israeli infrastructure and programs [1.2, 1.3].
*   **Taube Philanthropies:** Identified as a board member of Taube Philanthropies, Freidenrich oversees a mission that includes "strengthening ties between Jewish communities and Israel" and countering what the foundation describes as "unprecedented global threat[s] to the Jewish people" [1.4, 1.5].

#### Institutional Support for Global Jewish Identity Projects
Freidenrich is recognized as a "Key Donor" (contributing $1 million or more) to the **Association of the Jewish Historical Institute of Poland (POLIN Museum)** [1.6, 1.7]. This project, heavily supported by Taube Philanthropies, is designed to foster "Jewish Peoplehood" across the United States, Poland, and Israel, frequently facilitating exchange programs between Polish and Israeli youth to solidify Zionist ties [1.8].
