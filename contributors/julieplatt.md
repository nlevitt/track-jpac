# Contributor Report: Platt, Julie

### Overview
Julie Platt (née Beren) is a prominent American political donor and communal leader who served as the Chair of the Board of Trustees for **The Jewish Federations of North America (JFNA)** from 2022 to 2025 ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Julie_Platt); [JFNA](https://www.jewishfederations.org/news/jewish-federations-chair-julie-platt-receives-israeli-presidential-medal)). In this capacity, she presided over the largest Jewish philanthropic network in North America, which oversees 146 federations and 300 communities distributing over $3 billion annually ([JFNA Mission](https://www.jewishfederations.org/who-we-are)). Platt’s documented record includes substantial financial contributions to pro-Israel lobbying groups, leadership in massive pro-Israel public demonstrations, and the coordination of nearly $1 billion in emergency funding for the State of Israel.

---

### Documented Relationship to Israel and Zionist Advocacy

#### 1. Financial Contributions and Pledges
*   **AIPAC Pledge:** In the immediate aftermath of October 7, 2023, Platt pledged **$500,000** to the **American Israel Public Affairs Committee (AIPAC)** as part of a $90 million fundraising surge intended to bolster the organization's pro-Israel political influence ([The Forward](https://forward.com/news/580173/aipac-90-million-fundraising-oct-7/)).
*   **Jewish Future Pledge:** Platt was an early signatory of the **Jewish Future Pledge**, a formal commitment to designate at least 50% of her charitable estate to Jewish and/or Israel-related causes ([Jerusalem Post](https://www.jpost.com/jewish-world/article-729487)).
*   **Institutional Lobbying:** As Chair of JFNA, Platt oversaw an organization that spent approximately **$750,000 in 2023** and **$320,000 in 2024** on federal lobbying efforts ([OpenSecrets via InfluenceWatch](https://www.influencewatch.org/non-profit/jewish-federations-of-north-america-jfna/)). These efforts prioritized "guaranteeing support for Israel" and securing "military assistance needed to be victorious" ([JFNA Public Policy](https://www.jewishfederations.org/public-policy)).

#### 2. Leadership in Zionist Advocacy Organizations
*   **Chair of JFNA:** Platt’s core mission at JFNA included advocating for a "robust US-Israel relationship" and opposing the **Boycott, Divestment, and Sanctions (BDS)** movement ([JFNA Public Policy](https://www.jewishfederations.org/public-policy)).
*   **Israel Emergency Fund:** Following the October 7 attacks, Platt led the JFNA's **Israel Emergency Fund**, which raised **$855 million** for humanitarian and recovery efforts in Israel, the largest campaign in the organization's history ([Jewish Federation of Macon](https://www.jewishmacon.org/news/jewish-federations-chair-julie-platt-receives-israeli-presidential-medal); [eJewishPhilanthropy](https://ejewishphilanthropy.com/what-we-can-learn-from-julie-beren-platts-legacy-at-jfna/)).
*   **The Jewish Agency for Israel:** Platt serves on the Board of Governors of **The Jewish Agency**, an organization funded by JFNA and the World Zionist Organization, which focuses on facilitating Aliyah (immigration to Israel) and strengthening Zionist identity ([Jewish Agency Profile](https://www.jewishagency.org/julie-platt/)).

#### 3. Public Advocacy and Events
*   **March for Israel:** Platt co-hosted and was a featured speaker at the **March for Israel** on November 14, 2023, in Washington, D.C. The event, which drew approximately 290,000 people, was the largest pro-Israel rally in U.S. history and focused on demanding the release of hostages and demonstrating solidarity with the Israeli government ([JFNA News](https://www.jewishfederations.org/news/nearly-300000-gather-for-largest-pro-israel-rally-in-history)).
*   **University of Pennsylvania Leadership:** While serving as Vice Chair (and later Interim Chair) of the University of Pennsylvania Board of Trustees, Platt issued statements expressing "solidarity with all those who have been impacted by the horrific assault on Israel" and advocated for the university to take "unprecedented steps" to combat anti-Israel activism on campus ([UPenn Official Statement](https://freedomofexpression.upenn.edu/supporting-our-community/julie-platt-statement/)).

#### 4. Recognition by the State of Israel
*   **Presidential Medal of Honor:** In January 2025, Israeli President **Isaac Herzog** awarded Platt the **Israeli Presidential Medal of Honor**, the country's highest civilian award. The citation recognized her "unwavering commitment to Israel" and her leadership in ensuring Israel has "what it needs to help her people rebuild" ([Jerusalem Post](https://www.jpost.com/israel-news/article-837424); [JFNA Press Release](https://www.jewishfederations.org/news/jewish-federations-chair-julie-platt-receives-israeli-presidential-medal)).
