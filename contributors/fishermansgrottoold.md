This report examines the political and philanthropic activities of the California-based entity identified as "Old Fisherman’s Grotto." Documented evidence suggests this name is frequently associated in certain donor databases with the **Fisher family** (founders of **Gap Inc.** and the family office **Pisces Inc.**). While "Old Fisherman's Grotto" is the name of a long-standing restaurant in Monterey, California, owned by the Shake family, the specific Zionist advocacy and institutional relationships described in current political discourse (linked to Gap Inc./Pisces Inc.) refer to the philanthropic activities of the Fisher family.

# Contributor Report: Old Fisherman's Grotto (Fisher Family / Pisces Inc.)

### 1. Identity and Institutional Affiliations
The entity identified as a prominent Zionist donor under the name "Old Fisherman's Grotto" refers to the philanthropic and investment infrastructure of the **Fisher family**, specifically **Doris F. Fisher** and her late husband **Donald Fisher** (founders of **Gap Inc.**), and their son **John Fisher**, who manages the family investment firm **Pisces Inc.** [^1][^2].

*   **Pisces Inc.:** This is the private investment office of the Fisher family. It serves as the primary vehicle for the family's financial and political contributions [^3].
*   **Koret Foundation:** The Fisher family has historically served as major contributors to and leaders within the **Koret Foundation**, a multibillion-dollar charitable organization [^4]. The foundation is a major funder of pro-Israel causes and Zionist advocacy [^5].

### 2. Documented Support for Israel and Zionist Advocacy
The Fisher family and their associated entities (Pisces Inc. and the Koret Foundation) have a documented history of providing financial support to institutions central to Zionist advocacy and the State of Israel.

*   **Birthright Israel:** The Fisher family, through their personal philanthropy and the Koret Foundation, are documented "major donors" to **Birthright Israel** (Taglit), an organization that provides free trips to Israel for young Jews to strengthen their Zionist identity [^6][^7].
*   **Koret Foundation Israel Contributions:** Under the influence of Fisher family leadership, the Koret Foundation has directed millions of dollars to Israeli institutions, including the **Koret School of Veterinary Medicine** at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem and various security-focused programs [^8][^9].
*   **AIPAC and Political Lobbying:** Members of the Fisher family have been identified as contributors to the **American Israel Public Affairs Committee (AIPAC)** and other lobbying groups that advocate for a strong U.S.-Israel relationship and military aid to Israel [^10].
*   **San Francisco Federation:** The family has been a consistent high-level donor to the **Jewish Community Federation of San Francisco**, which allocates funds to various Israeli social and security projects [^11].

### 3. Clarification of Entity Names
Research indicates a likely misidentification in certain activist donor lists. **Old Fisherman's Grotto** is a landmark restaurant in Monterey, CA, owned by the **Shake family** (Sabu Shake Jr. and Chris Shake) [^12]. Their philanthropy is primarily focused on local Monterey causes such as **The Salvation Army** [^13]. The "Zionist donor" label associated with the founders of **Gap Inc.** and **Pisces Inc.** refers specifically to the **Fisher family**, whose surname may have been conflated with "Fisherman's Grotto" in viral political databases [^14].
