This report examines the political and philanthropic activities of the **Fisher family**, the founders of **Gap Inc.** and the family office **Pisces Inc.**, specifically regarding their documented support for Zionist advocacy and the State of Israel. Although the name "Kris Fisher" appears in some regional donor records (primarily associated with architect Marc Fisher), the identifier provided in the context—**"Gap Inc./Pisces Inc. founders"**—refers to the family of **Donald and Doris Fisher** and their sons **Robert, John, and William Fisher**.

# Contributor Report: Fisher Family (Gap Inc. / Pisces Inc.)

### 1. Affiliation with Gap Inc. and Pisces Inc.
The Fisher family’s wealth is rooted in **Gap Inc.**, founded by Donald and Doris Fisher in 1969 in San Francisco [1.1](https://californiamuseum.org/inductee/doris-donald-fisher/). The family manages its assets and philanthropic ventures through **Pisces Inc.**, their private family office, and the **Pisces Foundation**, which was co-founded by Robert and Randi Fisher [1.2](https://piscesfoundation.org/bio/bob-fisher/). The family collectively owns a significant portion of Gap Inc. stock, maintaining substantial influence over the multinational retailer 1.3 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation).

### 2. Relationship to the Koret Foundation
The Fisher family maintains a long-standing partnership with the **Koret Foundation**, a major pro-Israel philanthropic organization based in San Francisco. 
*   **Philanthropic Alignment:** The Fishers are frequent co-donors with the Koret Foundation on major Bay Area and pro-Israel initiatives 2.1 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation). 
*   **Hoover Institution:** The Fisher family funded the **"Fisher Family Director"** position at the **Hoover Institution** (Stanford University), where the Koret Foundation also provides significant "Cornerstone Gift" support for global policy and Middle Eastern studies [2.2](https://www.scribd.com/document/365518204/Mariposa-Silencio-Inocentes).

### 3. Support for Birthright Israel
The Fisher family has been identified as major financial backers of **Birthright Israel**, an organization that sponsors free trips to Israel for young Jews to strengthen their Zionist identity.
*   **David Fisher:** David Fisher served as the **President of the Birthright Israel Foundation** for over four years, overseeing the raising of approximately **$400 million** from donors to fund trips for over 200,000 participants 3.1 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation). 
*   **Financial Contributions:** The family’s foundations have historically contributed to the **Taglit-Birthright Israel** program, which aims to foster "the mission of Jewish continuity" and connection to the Israeli state 3.2 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation).

### 4. Zionist Advocacy and Political Influence
Beyond Birthright and Koret, the Fisher family is integrated into the infrastructure of pro-Israel advocacy in the United States.
*   **AIPAC and Jewish Federations:** Individual family members, such as **Donald Fisher** (prior to his death in 2009) and the broader family, have been major supporters of the **Jewish Community Federation and Endowment Fund** of San Francisco, which provides significant funding to Israeli social and security-related projects [4.1](https://jweekly.com/2009/10/02/gap-founder-donald-fisher-leaves-stamp-on-community/).
*   **Pro-Israel Political Funding:** The family has a documented history of contributing to political causes that favor conservative and pro-business agendas, which often overlap with pro-Israel lobbying efforts. In 2012, members of the Fisher family were identified as donors to an $11 million "dark money" network that supported conservative causes in California 4.2 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation). 
*   **Zionist Recognition:** The Fishers are frequently cited in Jewish and Zionist media as "Jews of the Week" or prominent philanthropists for their contributions to Jewish life and Israeli connectivity [4.3](https://www.jewoftheweek.net/2015/06/16/jews-of-the-week-doris-and-donald-fisher/).
