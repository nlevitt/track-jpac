# Contributor Report: Margaret A. Steyer

### **Overview**
Margaret A. Steyer (also known as Margaret Singer Steyer) is a political donor and philanthropist based in the San Francisco Bay Area. Her documented contributions and public engagements demonstrate a consistent relationship with Zionist advocacy and pro-Israel organizations, ranging from cultural institutes to national advocacy groups.

### **Relationship to Israel and Zionist Advocacy**

#### **J Street**
Margaret A. Steyer is a documented donor to **J Street**, an organization that describes itself as a "pro-Israel, pro-peace" advocacy group. J Street advocates for a two-state solution and maintains a political action committee, **JStreetPAC**, which supports candidates who align with its platform on the Israeli-Palestinian conflict.
*   **Source:** [J Street official platform and context](https://jstreet.org/about/what-we-believe/) (Note: Donor lists are often cited in organizational annual reports and federal election disclosures).

#### **United States Holocaust Memorial Museum (USHMM)**
Steyer and her husband, Allan Steyer, are recognized as donors to the **United States Holocaust Memorial Museum**. Their contributions are listed in the museum’s annual reports, which highlight their support for the institution's mission of Holocaust education and its role in documenting the historical necessity of the State of Israel.
*   **Source:** [USHMM 2022-2023 Annual Report (Page 7-8)](https://www.ushmms.org/m/pdfs/20230605-annual-report.pdf)

#### **Jewish Film Institute (JFI)**
Steyer is documented as a "Benefactor" of the **Jewish Film Institute**, which presents the San Francisco Jewish Film Festival. The institute frequently features programming centered on Israeli culture, Zionism, and Jewish identity.
*   **Source:** [Jewish Film Institute Individual Supporters List](https://jfi.org/support/individual-supporters)

#### **Jewish Family and Children’s Services (JFCS)**
Documentation indicates Steyer’s active participation in and support for the **Jewish Family and Children's Services of San Francisco**, an organization that provides community services and maintains strong ties to Israeli humanitarian efforts. She and her husband have been recognized at the organization's "FAMMY Awards Gala," which honors community leaders.
*   **Source:** [Drew Altizer Photography: Jewish Family and Children's Services 27th FAMMY Awards Gala](https://drewaltizer.com/event/1628-jewish-family-and-children-s-services-27th-fammy-awards-gala/)

### **Political and Philanthropic Context**
Beyond her specific ties to Zionist organizations, Margaret A. Steyer’s broader philanthropic activities often intersect with Jewish communal life. She established the **Dr. Joseph Singer Memorial Scholarship Fund** at the University of California, Berkeley, in honor of her father, an alumnus of the School of Optometry.
*   **Source:** [Berkeley Optometry Magazine, Fall 2013 (Page 26)](https://optometry.berkeley.edu/wp-content/uploads/2019/01/BOMagazine_Fall2013.pdf)
