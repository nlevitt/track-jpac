# Contributor Report: Fisher, Kevin W.

### Identity and Background
The name **Kevin W. Fisher** is frequently associated in political and activist databases with the **Fisher family of San Francisco**, specifically the founders of **Gap Inc.** and the principals of the family office **Pisces Inc.** While the founders of Gap Inc. are **Donald Fisher** (d. 2009) and **Doris F. Fisher**, their three sons—**Robert J. Fisher**, **William S. Fisher**, and **John J. Fisher**—manage the family's commercial and philanthropic interests through **Pisces Inc.** and the **Pisces Foundation** ([Forbes](https://www.forbes.com/profile/robert-fisher/), [Gap Inc.](https://www.gapinc.com/en-us/about/history)). The family is based in California and is consistently identified as a major source of political and philanthropic funding for pro-Israel causes ([SFGate](https://www.sfgate.com/news/article/WEALTH-POWER-AND-VISION-Gap-founder-Don-Fisher-2509176.php)).

### Relationship to Israel and Zionist Advocacy
The Fisher family is documented as a core financial pillar for several prominent Zionist and pro-Israel organizations. Their advocacy is characterized by large-scale institutional support and strategic leadership roles.

#### 1. Support for Birthright Israel
The Fisher family, primarily through **Doris Fisher** and **John J. Fisher**, are longstanding "major donors" to the **Birthright Israel Foundation**. This organization funds 10-day "heritage trips" to Israel for young Jewish adults, a program explicitly designed to strengthen Zionist identity and connection to the State of Israel (Birthright Israel Foundation (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)). 
*   **Documentation:** The Birthright Israel Foundation's "Scroll of Honor" consistently lists the Fisher family at the highest tiers of giving (frequently $1,000,000+ per year) (Issuu: Birthright Israel Foundation 2024 Scroll of Honor (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)).
*   **Leadership:** **David Fisher** (noted as part of the broader philanthropic network) previously served as the President of the Birthright Israel Foundation (Atlanta Jewish Times (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)).

#### 2. Koret Foundation
The Fisher family maintains a deep institutional relationship with the **Koret Foundation**, a San Francisco-based philanthropic organization with a significant focus on "strengthening Israel" and "Jewish peoplehood."
*   **Donations:** The Fishers are major contributors to Koret, which in turn provides tens of millions of dollars in grants to Israeli universities, security-related research, and programs that promote Israel’s standing internationally ([Koret Foundation: Israel](https://koret.org/grantees/israel-at-75-the-wonder-of-a-jewish-state/)).
*   **Collaboration:** The Koret Foundation and the Fisher family often co-fund projects in the San Francisco Bay Area and Israel, such as the **Jewish Community Federation and Endowment Fund**, which allocates significant resources to Israeli state projects ([J. Weekly](https://jweekly.com/2009/10/02/gap-founder-donald-fisher-leaves-stamp-on-community/)).

#### 3. Political Advocacy and AIPAC
The family's political contributions are heavily directed toward candidates and committees that support the U.S.-Israel alliance.
*   **AIPAC Connections:** **Isaac "Ike" Fisher**, often cited alongside other prominent Fisher family donors in pro-Israel advocacy circles, has served on the **Central Council of AIPAC** (American Israel Public Affairs Committee). In 2017, he famously threatened to "suspend" his multi-million dollar philanthropy to Israel as a "wake-up call" to the Israeli government over internal religious policies, highlighting the scale and leverage of his financial commitment ([Times of Israel](https://www.timesofisrael.com/angry-us-donor-wants-to-issue-a-wake-up-call-isnt-planning-to-pull-his-israel-funding/), [Ynet News](https://www.ynetnews.com/articles/0,7340,L-4983944,00.html)).
*   **California Political Contributions:** Members of the Gap-founding Fisher family are top individual donors in California, contributing millions to pro-Israel and conservative causes (LA Times (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)).
