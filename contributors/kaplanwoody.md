# Contributor Report: Kaplan, Woody

### Background and Identity
Woody Kaplan (1942–2023) was a Boston-based commercial real estate investor and a prolific political donor who focused on civil liberties, secular advocacy, and liberal Zionism. In his federal and state political filings, he frequently listed his occupation as "Provocateur." Kaplan was a founding member of the Secular Coalition for America and served on the national board of the American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU) and the Foundation for Individual Rights in Education (FIRE).

### Documented Relationship to Israel and Zionist Advocacy
Kaplan's support for Israel was primarily manifested through his financial and organizational involvement with **J Street**, an American organization that describes itself as "pro-Israel" and "pro-peace," advocating for a two-state solution and liberal Zionist principles.

*   **Major Donor to J Street:** Kaplan was identified as a major donor to J Street, an organization that lobbies for a "democratic and Jewish" Israel while supporting a negotiated end to the Israeli-Palestinian conflict.

*   **Affiliation with FIRE and Zionist Discourse:** As a board member of the Foundation for Individual Rights in Education (FIRE), Kaplan was involved in institutional advocacy regarding the boundaries of Zionist and anti-Zionist speech on American campuses.     *   **Source:** [FIRE Letter to Columbia University President Lee Bollinger (January 10, 2005)](https://www.thefire.org/research-learn/fire-letter-columbia-university-president-lee-bollinger-january-10-2005)

### Leadership and Philanthropic Context
Kaplan's pro-Israel advocacy was part of a broader portfolio of high-value political giving. He was a member of the "Tikva Society," a designation for J Street's major contributors who commit to multi-year investments in the organization's growth. His personal connection to Israel is also documented through family ties; one of his daughters, Abigail Kaplan, is a resident of Jerusalem.

*   **Source:** [J Street 2018 Financial Statement and Tikva Society Recognition](https://jstreet.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/04/J-Street-2018-Financial-Statement.pdf)
*   **Source:** [Legacy.com - Woody Kaplan Obituary](https://www.legacy.com/us/obituaries/bostonglobe/name/woody-kaplan-obituary?id=52622438)

### Summary of Zionist Advocacy
Woody Kaplan's relationship to Zionism was defined by his support for the liberal wing of the movement. His contributions were aimed at maintaining Israel’s status as a Jewish democracy through the promotion of a two-state solution, a position central to the J Street advocacy framework. His documented financial support made him a stakeholder in the effort to shift American policy toward a more diplomatic approach to the Israeli-Palestinian conflict.
