# Contributor Report: Susan Ellenberg for Supervisor 2026

Susan Ellenberg is a current member of the Santa Clara County Board of Supervisors representing District 4 and is a candidate for re-election in 2026. Her political career and personal background reflect a documented relationship with Zionist advocacy and pro-Israel organizations.

### 1. Political Endorsements and Institutional Support
Ellenberg has been formally endorsed by advocacy groups that prioritize support for Israel as a core component of their candidate evaluation.
*   **JPAC (Jewish Public Affairs Committee of California):** Ellenberg is identified as a candidate endorsed by JPAC, a prominent pro-Israel advocacy group in California. JPAC (recently rebranded as "Jewish California") functions as a leading advocate for the California Jewish community, focusing on deepening ties between California and Israel and opposing anti-BDS legislation.
    *   *Source:* [JPAC-Cal Official Site](https://jpac-cal.org/) (Referenced in multiple advocacy materials; see also: [Jewish Insider Candidate Forum Report](https://jewishinsider.com/2026/02/california-gubernatorial-candidates-pledge-israel-ties-fight-bds/)).
*   **Jewish Democratic Coalition of Silicon Valley (JDCSV):** Ellenberg has received multiple endorsements from the JDCSV, a "pro-Israel Club." The organization's endorsement process involves a questionnaire and interview ensuring candidates support Israel’s right to exist as a "Jewish and democratic state."
    *   *Source:* [JDCSV Endorsement Page](https://jdcsv.com/endorsements/)

### 2. Pro-Israel Advocacy and Political Identity
Public characterizations and official statements establish Ellenberg's long-term identification with Zionist causes.
*   **AIPAC and Zionist Labeling:** During her initial 2018 campaign for Supervisor, Ellenberg was explicitly characterized by local political media as a "Zionist" and an "AIPAC-supporter." This reporting noted that even constituents who differed on Middle East policy supported her due to these credentials.
    *   *Source:* [San Jose Inside (Nov 7, 2018)](https://www.sanjoseinside.com/news/susan-ellenberg-leads-in-santa-clara-county-supervisor-race/)
*   **Israel Solidarity Event (2023):** Following the October 7, 2023, attacks, Ellenberg spoke at an "Israel solidarity event" in Los Gatos organized by Jewish Silicon Valley. During her remarks, she emphasized her personal ties to Israel and her responsibility as a leader within the Jewish community.
    *   *Source:* [Los Gatan (Oct 30, 2023)](https://www.losgatan.com/israel-solidarity-event-draws-overflow-crowd-in-los-gatos/)

### 3. Personal and Professional Background in Israel
Ellenberg's professional history and education demonstrate a sustained connection to Israeli institutions.
*   **Educational Stays in Israel:** Ellenberg spent a semester studying at Tel Aviv University, a period she has described as influential to her personal development and Hebrew proficiency.
    *   *Source:* [Joint Venture Silicon Valley Board Profile](https://jointventure.org/news/joint-venture-profiles/2030-meet-supervisor-susan-ellenberg-santa-clara-county-district-4)
*   **Affiliation with Jewish Educational Institutions:** Prior to her supervisorial role, Ellenberg taught "social justice and civic engagement" for six years at the Yavneh Day School, a Jewish community school that integrates Jewish identity and support for Israel into its curriculum.
    *   *Source:* [Santa Clara County District 4 Official Bio](https://supervisorellenberg.santaclaracounty.gov/about-supervisor-ellenberg)

### 4. Legislative Stance on Regional Conflicts
In her capacity as Board President and Supervisor, Ellenberg has faced significant public pressure regarding the Israel-Gaza conflict.
*   **Ceasefire Resolution Resistance:** Activists and constituents have documented efforts to pressure Ellenberg to place a Gaza ceasefire resolution on the Board of Supervisors' agenda. Correspondence from advocacy groups highlights a perceived lack of urgency or refusal to prioritize such resolutions relative to other county business.
    *   *Source:* [City of Palo Alto Public Records / Aram James Archives (March 8, 2024)](https://www.cityofpaloalto.org/files/assets/public/v/1/agendas-and-minutes/human-relations-commission/2024/04-11-2024/hrc-04.11.2024-public-comments.pdf)
