# Contributor Report: Swig, Roselyne C.

Roselyne C. Swig is a long-standing leader in several prominent Jewish and Zionist advocacy organizations. Her documented relationship with Israel is defined by high-level governance roles in lobbying groups, substantial philanthropic support for Israeli institutions, and public advocacy for U.S.-Israel relations.

### 1. Organizational Leadership and Governance
Swig holds emeritus and lifetime positions in major organizations that focus on political advocacy for Israel and Jewish communal interests.

*   **AIPAC (American Israel Public Affairs Committee):** Swig serves as the **National Vice President Emeritus** of AIPAC, the primary pro-Israel lobbying organization in the United States. AIPAC's mission includes lobbying the U.S. government to enact policies that strengthen the U.S.-Israel relationship.
    *   *Source:* [U.S. Commission for the Preservation of America’s Heritage Abroad](https://www.heritageabroad.gov/about/commissioners/roselyne-chroman-swig)
    *   *Source:* [Vital Voices Global Partnership](https://www.vitalvoices.org/people/roselyne-swig/)
*   **Shalom Hartman Institute (SHI):** She is a **Lifetime Trustee** of this Jerusalem-based research and education institute. SHI works to strengthen Jewish peoplehood and the "Jewish and democratic" character of the State of Israel.
    *   *Source:* [Shalom Hartman Institute Board List](https://www.hartman.org.il/person/cissie-swig/)
*   **Jewish Community Relations Council (JCRC) Bay Area:** Swig is an **Emeritus Board Member** of the JCRC, an organization that maintains a central focus on "advocating for Israel" and countering movements like Boycott, Divestment, and Sanctions (BDS).
    *   *Source:* [JCRC Bay Area Board & Leadership](https://www.jcrc.org/who-we-are/)
*   **Jewish Community Federation and Endowment Fund:** She is a **Past President** (1992–1994) and current board member of the San Francisco-based Federation, which allocates millions of dollars annually to programs in Israel and initiatives to strengthen Israel-diaspora ties.
    *   *Source:* [J. Weekly Profile](https://www.jweekly.com/2019/03/07/cissie-swig-still-a-visionary-always-a-supporter-of-good-causes/)

### 2. Documented Zionist Advocacy and Philanthropy
Swig’s relationship to Israel is further documented through numerous high-profile awards and dedicated funding initiatives.

*   **State of Israel Bonds:** Swig has received the **Woman of Valor Award** and the **Golda Meir Leadership Award** from the State of Israel Bonds, an organization that facilitates investment in Israeli infrastructure and development. The Golda Meir Award was the first of its kind authorized by the former Prime Minister’s family.
    *   *Source:* [National Library of Israel - J. Weekly Archive (1986)](https://www.nli.org.il/en/newspapers/jwnc/1986/10/31/01/article/107)
*   **A Wider Bridge:** In 2023, she was honored with the **Tzedek Award** by A Wider Bridge, an organization that advocates for LGBTQ rights in Israel and promotes "Zionist LGBTQ" identity in the United States. During her acceptance, she reflected on her involvement with Israel since its founding.
    *   *Source:* [Jerusalem Post - A Wider Bridge Gala Report](https://www.jpost.com/diaspora/article-734394)
*   **Roselyne C. Swig Tikkun Olam Innovation Prize:** This prize was established in her honor to support social and technological innovation. It has been used to fund startups and initiatives within the Israeli innovation ecosystem.
    *   *Source:* [J. Weekly - Innovation Alley and the Swig Prize](https://www.jweekly.com/2013/06/07/the-column-your-dollars-in-israel-making-world-better/)
*   **Support for Israeli Defense and Social Integration:** Through her board roles at the **American Jewish Joint Distribution Committee (JDC)** and the SF Federation, Swig has supported programs such as *Shachar Chadash*, which facilitates the integration of ultra-Orthodox men into the Israeli military (IDF).
    *   *Source:* [J. Weekly - IDF Integration Funding](https://www.jweekly.com/2013/06/07/the-column-your-dollars-in-israel-making-world-better/)

### 3. Public Positions on U.S.-Israel Relations
Swig maintains an active role in shaping communal responses to Israeli political and social developments.

*   **Political Engagement:** In public forums, Swig has emphasized the necessity of a "stable" and secure Israel, occasionally commenting on internal Israeli legal reforms as they pertain to the country's international standing and its relationship with the American Jewish community.
    *   *Source:* [Jerusalem Post - Acceptance Speech Coverage](https://www.jpost.com/diaspora/article-734394)
*   **Opposition to De-legitimization:** As a leader in the JCRC and Federation, she has participated in crafting and supporting policies that restrict funding for organizations that endorse the BDS movement or otherwise "undermine the legitimacy of Israel."
    *   *Source:* [Forward - SF Federation Funding Guidelines](https://forward.com/israel/128148/debate-over-israel-funding-guidelines-continues-to/)
