# Contributor Report: Google LLC and Affiliated Entities (Mark Isakowitz)

### Overview
Google LLC, an American multinational technology company under the parent company Alphabet Inc., maintains significant economic, technological, and political ties to the State of Israel. Mark Isakowitz, Google’s Vice President of Government Affairs and Public Policy for the Americas, serves as a primary liaison between the corporation and political entities, including pro-Israel lobbying organizations. The relationship is characterized by high-level participation in lobbying conferences, large-scale government contracts with the Israeli military, and strategic infrastructure projects within Israel.

### Documented Support for Israel and Zionist Advocacy

#### 1. Participation in AIPAC Programming
Mark Isakowitz has represented Google as a speaker at the American Israel Public Affairs Committee (AIPAC) Policy Conference, a primary venue for pro-Israel advocacy in the United States. In 2019, Isakowitz participated as a featured speaker on a panel titled "The Future of Innovation: How Israel and America Can Change the World," which focused on deepening technological cooperation between the two nations.
*   **Source:** [AIPAC 2019 Policy Conference Program Guide (Page 36)](https://digital.copcomm.com/i/1090176-2019-policy-conference-program-guide/35?)

#### 2. Project Nimbus: Military and Government Cloud Contract
Google, in partnership with Amazon, entered into a $1.2 billion contract known as "Project Nimbus" to provide cloud computing services to the Israeli government and the Israel Defense Forces (IDF). Documentation indicates the contract prohibits Google from terminating services to specific government entities, ensuring continuous support for the Israeli military apparatus despite internal and external protests regarding the use of AI in surveillance and military operations.
*   **Source:** [The Intercept: Google and Amazon Face Internal Revolt Over $1.2 Billion Israel Contract](https://theintercept.com/2021/10/12/google-amazon-israel-nimbus-air-force-contract/)
*   **Source:** [TIME: Google Workers Protesting $1.2 Billion Israel Contract Fired](https://time.com/6967527/google-protest-israel-project-nimbus-fired/)

#### 3. Strategic Infrastructure and the "Blue-Raman" Subsea Cable
Google has invested in the "Blue-Raman" subsea cable project, which connects Europe to India via Israel. By routing this critical data infrastructure through the Negev desert, Google integrates Israel as a central hub for global telecommunications, a move noted for its geopolitical significance in normalizing and strengthening Israel's regional economic position.
*   **Source:** [TechCrunch: Google to Build Subsea Cable Connecting Italy and India via Israel](https://techcrunch.com/2021/07/29/google-to-build-subsea-cable-connecting-italy-and-india-via-israel/)
*   **Source:** [Wall Street Journal: Google Is Planning a New Subsea Cable to Connect India and Europe via Israel](https://www.wsj.com/articles/google-is-planning-a-new-undersea-cable-to-connect-india-and-europe-11606140001)

#### 4. Research and Development Expansion in Israel
Google maintains a robust physical presence in Israel, including major R&D centers in Tel Aviv and Haifa. The company consistently expands these facilities, which work closely with Israeli academic institutions and start-ups, contributing to the "Start-Up Nation" economic framework central to Israeli state branding and economic sustainability.
*   **Source:** [Reuters: Google to Invest $25 Million in Israeli Tech Skills and Integration](https://www.reuters.com/technology/google-invest-25-mln-israeli-tech-skills-integration-2022-02-20/)
*   **Source:** [The Times of Israel: Google CEO Announces New $25 Million Initiative in Israel](https://www.timesofisrael.com/google-ceo-unveils-25m-initiative-to-boost-israels-tech-workforce/)

#### 5. Political Contributions to Pro-Israel Candidates
Through its political action committee (Google Inc. NetPAC) and individual contributions from executives like Isakowitz, Google directs funds to U.S. legislators who are key proponents of military aid to Israel and who receive substantial backing from AIPAC and the Democratic Majority for Israel (DMFI).
