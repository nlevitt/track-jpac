# Contributor Report: Bronfman, Samuel

Samuel Bronfman (1889–1971) was a Canadian businessman and the founder of the Seagram Company who served in high-level leadership positions within international and domestic Zionist organizations. His documented relationship to Israel is characterized by substantial financial contributions, the facilitation of armaments procurement, and the coordination of pro-Israel political advocacy through his roles in the World Jewish Congress (WJC) and the Canadian Jewish Congress (CJC).

### 1. Organizational Leadership and Zionist Advocacy
Bronfman held primary leadership roles in organizations that maintain a pro-Israel stance:
*   **World Jewish Congress (WJC):** Bronfman was a Vice President of the World Jewish Congress and chairman of its Western Hemisphere executive. These roles involved coordinating global advocacy for Jewish causes, including support for the State of Israel.

    *   **Source:** [Jewish Telegraphic Agency - W.J.C. Drafts Program](https://www.jta.org/archive/w-j-c-drafts-program-on-threat-of-neo-nazism-in-germany-jewish-communities-to-study-it)
*   **Canadian Jewish Congress (CJC):** He served as President of the CJC from 1939 to 1962, during which he shifted the organization's focus toward Zionist state-building and financial support for Israel.

### 2. Financial and Material Support for Israel
Bronfman provided direct material and financial assistance to the Israeli government and its military establishment:
*   **1951 Arms Procurement:** In 1951, Bronfman assisted Shimon Peres (then head of procurement for Israel's defense ministry) in purchasing $2 million worth of weapons from the Canadian government at a 50% discount. When Israel was unable to pay the remaining $1 million, Bronfman organized a fundraising dinner to cover the costs himself.
    *   **Source:** [Entrepreneur.com - Mr. Sam the Whiskey Man](https://www.entrepreneur.com/growing-business/mr-sam-the-whiskey-man/197584)
    *   **Source:** [DOKUMEN.PUB - Leo: A Life (Biography Reference)](https://dokumen.pub/leo-a-life-9780773571570.html)
*   **Israel Emergency Campaign:** In June 1967, following the Six-Day War, Bronfman announced a Canada-wide fundraising goal of $20,000,000 for the Israel Emergency Campaign to provide material assistance to the state.
    *   **Source:** [Jewish Telegraphic Agency - Bronfman Announces $20M Goal](https://www.jta.org/archive/bronfman-announces-20000000-canada-wide-goal-for-israel-emergency-campaign)
*   **Institutional Funding:** Bronfman financed various infrastructure and cultural projects in Israel, including the Samuel Bronfman Museum in Jerusalem and agricultural research initiatives.
    *   **Source:** [Jewish Telegraphic Agency - Bronfman Son Pledges Support](https://www.jta.org/archive/bronfman-son-pledges-continued-support-for-israel)

### 3. Political Recognition and Institutional Legacy
His advocacy and financial support for the Zionist cause were formally recognized by international governments and the institutions he established:
*   **Légion d’honneur for Zionism:** The government of France awarded Bronfman the Chevalier de la Légion d’honneur specifically in recognition of his support for the Zionist movement.
    *   **Source:** [EBSCO - Samuel Bronfman Biography](https://www.ebsco.com/sites/default/files/reese-starters/Samuel-Bronfman.pdf)
*   **The Samuel Bronfman Foundation:** The foundation established in his name operates under guiding principles that include the "unity of the Jewish people" throughout the world and in Israel, and it continues to fund Zionist educational programs such as the Bronfman Fellowships in Israel.
    *   **Source:** [The Samuel Bronfman Foundation - Our Mission](https://www.thesbf.org/about/mission/)
    *   **Source:** [Wikipedia - Bronfman Fellowship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Bronfman_Fellowship)
