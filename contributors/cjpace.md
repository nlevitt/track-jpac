# Contributor Report: PACE, J.C. (Jewish Public Affairs Committee of California)

### 1. Entity Identification and Mission
The entity identified in political contribution and advocacy records as "PACE, J.C." or "J.C. PACE" is the **Jewish Public Affairs Committee of California (JPAC)**, which recently rebranded as **Jewish California** in early 2026. Founded in 1972, it serves as the largest statewide coalition of Jewish community organizations in the United States, representing over 41 member groups, including regional Jewish Federations, Jewish Community Relations Councils (JCRCs), and national organizations such as the Anti-Defamation League (ADL) and the American Jewish Committee (AJC).

*   **Primary Identity:** Jewish Public Affairs Committee of California (JPAC). [Source: Jewish California Official Site](https://www.jewishcal.org/about)
*   **Mission:** To amplify Jewish voices in Sacramento through strategic advocacy, focusing on pillars that include fighting antisemitism and "maintaining a strong California-Israel relationship." Source: Wikipedia (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)

### 2. Zionist Policy Framework
JPAC operates under a policy framework that explicitly prioritizes the defense of Israel and the promotion of Zionist objectives within California’s legislative and budgetary processes.

*   **California-Israel Relationship:** A core pillar of JPAC’s advocacy is to "maintain a strong California-Israel relationship" and "promote economic and cultural ties" between the state and Israel. Source: JWeekly (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)
*   **Counter-Delegitimization:** JPAC officially advocates against campaigns to "delegitimize and demonize" the State of Israel, specifically targeting the Boycott, Divestment, and Sanctions (BDS) movement and similar pro-Palestinian initiatives. [Source: Truthout](https://truthout.org/articles/california-ethnic-studies-bill-aims-to-censor-palestine-related-education/)

### 3. Documented Zionist Advocacy and Legislation
JPAC translates its Zionist framework into legislative action by sponsoring bills aimed at regulating education and defining antisemitism in ways that encompass criticism of Israel.

*   **Assembly Bill 715 (AB 715):** JPAC was the lead sponsor of this "landmark" legislation, signed into law in October 2025. The bill establishes a statewide "antisemitism coordinator" for K-12 schools and implements rules to remove content deemed "antisemitic" from ethnic studies curricula. This includes materials that pro-Israel organizations characterize as "denying the right of Israel to exist" or comparing Israeli policies to Nazism. [Source: San Diego Jewish World](https://www.sdjewishworld.com/2025/10/21/csu-faculty-union-asks-political-candidates-to-reject-jewish-campaign-contributions/)
*   **Assembly Bill 1468 (AB 1468):** JPAC has been documented as a primary sponsor of legislation intended to intervene in ethnic studies classrooms to "silence Palestine-related speech." Critics argue these bills aim to censor Palestinian historical narratives and the discussion of Israeli state violence. [Source: Truthout](https://truthout.org/articles/california-ethnic-studies-bill-aims-to-censor-palestine-related-education/)
*   **Legislative Jewish Caucus Relationship:** JPAC maintains a "preexisting relationship" and works in close coordination with the California Legislative Jewish Caucus, a pro-Israel bloc of lawmakers that exercises significant leverage in the State Assembly and Senate. Source: Wikipedia (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)

### 5. Critical Reception and Controversy
JPAC’s Zionist advocacy has led to direct political confrontations with labor unions and educational associations in California.

*   **CFA "Harmful" Designation:** In late 2025, the **California Faculty Association (CFA)**, representing 29,000 faculty, included JPAC in a candidate questionnaire alongside the American Israel Public Affairs Committee (AIPAC), the tobacco industry, and the oil industry. The questionnaire asked candidates to reject donations or endorsements from these groups, labeling them as entities that "harm working families." [Source: Golden Gate Xpress](https://goldengatexpress.org/105388/news/campus/a-look-into-sfsu-student-housing/attachment/capstone_pratap_studenthousing_p_1/)
*   **Conflict over Ethnic Studies:** JPAC’s lobbying for the "scrubbing" of Palestinian experiences from the 2021 ethnic studies model curriculum and its sponsorship of subsequent restrictive legislation has been characterized by academic critics as a "years-long attack on ethnic studies" intended to protect Israeli interests from academic scrutiny. [Source: Truthout](https://truthout.org/articles/california-ethnic-studies-bill-aims-to-censor-palestine-related-education/)
