The name **Donna J. Fisher** as a "founder" of Gap Inc. or Pisces Inc. appears to be a misidentification of **Doris F. Fisher** (and her late husband **Donald G. Fisher**). Public records and corporate documentation identify Doris and Donald Fisher as the founders of Gap Inc. and Pisces Inc. (the family's investment vehicle and foundation). The following report details the documented relationship between the Fisher family (principally Doris Fisher and the Fisher family foundations) and their support for Zionist advocacy and the State of Israel.

# Contributor Report: FISHER, DONNA J.

**Note:** Documentation suggests this entity refers to **Doris F. Fisher** (co-founder of Gap Inc. and Pisces Inc.) and the broader Fisher family philanthropic apparatus.

### 1. Overview of Identity and Corporate Affiliations
Donna J. Fisher (identified in donor lists as a surrogate for Doris F. Fisher) is a co-founder of **Gap Inc.** and **Pisces Inc.** (the family’s private investment firm and philanthropic foundation). The Fisher family's wealth, derived from Gap Inc. brands (Old Navy, Banana Republic, Athleta), has been directed through various entities to support pro-Israel causes.
*   **Gap Inc. / Pisces Inc.:** Doris and Donald Fisher founded Gap Inc. in 1969. Pisces Inc. serves as the family's investment management company and foundation arm. [Source: Forbes Profile](https://www.forbes.com/profile/doris-fisher/), [Gap Inc. History](https://www.gapinc.com/en-us/about/history).
*   **Political Context:** The Fisher family is among the most prominent political donors in California, often supporting candidates and initiatives aligned with pro-Israel lobbying groups. Source: Los Angeles Times Investigation (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation).

### 2. Documented Relationship to Zionist Advocacy
The Fisher family's philanthropic and political activities reflect a long-standing commitment to Zionist causes, primarily through the funding of organizations that promote Jewish identity connected to the State of Israel and institutional support for Israeli infrastructure.

#### Birthright Israel
Doris and Donald Fisher were among the "founding partners" and early major donors to **Birthright Israel**, an organization that provides free trips to Israel for young Jewish adults to strengthen their connection to the state and its Zionist ideology.
*   **Funding:** The Fisher family, through their personal wealth and foundations, has contributed millions of dollars to the Birthright Israel Foundation. They are frequently listed in the "Chairman's Council" ($1 million+ donors) or as "Founders" in official donor rolls. Source: Birthright Israel Foundation Annual Report Archive (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation).

#### Koret Foundation
The Fisher family maintains a deeply documented association with the **Koret Foundation**, one of the largest pro-Israel philanthropic organizations in the United States.
*   **Strategic Support:** Doris Fisher and her family have collaborated with the Koret Foundation on numerous initiatives designed to strengthen the U.S.-Israel relationship and support Zionist cultural projects in San Francisco and Israel. Source: Koret Foundation Grants List (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation).
*   **Controversy:** The Koret Foundation has been criticized by advocacy groups for its support of organizations that fund illegal Israeli settlements and provide material support to the Israeli military. Source: AFSC Investigate - Koret Foundation Profile (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation).

#### Jewish Community Federation and Endowment Fund (S.F.)
The Fishers have been primary contributors to the **Jewish Community Federation of San Francisco**, which directs substantial funding to Israeli projects, including those beyond the Green Line.
*   **Legacy Giving:** Donald Fisher was a former board member, and the family continues to be among the federation's top-tier donors. [Source: J. Weekly Obituary and Philanthropy Review](https://jweekly.com/2009/10/02/gap-founder-donald-fisher-leaves-stamp-on-community/).

### 3. Support for Israeli Institutions
The Fisher family has extended direct support to Israeli academic and social institutions that contribute to the state's soft power and infrastructure.
*   **Tel Aviv University:** The family has funded various programs and scholarships at Tel Aviv University, contributing to the academic standing of the institution. Source: Tel Aviv University Global Campaign (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation).
*   **Israel Bonds:** The family has historically been a significant purchaser of Israel Bonds, providing direct financial support to the Israeli government’s treasury. Source: Times of Israel Philanthropy Review (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation).

### 4. Summary of Political Contributions
Political records show that individuals associated with the Fisher family (including Doris and her sons Robert, William, and John) are frequent contributors to political action committees (PACs) that advocate for pro-Israel policies in Washington, D.C.
*   **Donor Records:** Contributions are documented to entities such as the American Israel Public Affairs Committee (AIPAC) and candidates who prioritize the maintenance of U.S. military aid to Israel.
