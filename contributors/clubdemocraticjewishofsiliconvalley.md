This research report details the platform and advocacy activities of the **Jewish Democratic Club of Silicon Valley** (JDCSV), also operating as the **Jewish Democratic Coalition of the Bay Area** (JDCBA). The organization is a chartered club within the California Democratic Party (CADEM) and functions as a regional affiliate of the broader **California Jewish Democrats** (formerly Democrats for Israel California).

# Contributor Report: Jewish Democratic Club of Silicon Valley

### 1. Core Mission and Platform on Israel
The Jewish Democratic Club of Silicon Valley (JDCSV) identifies its primary purpose as advocating for the "defense of a democratic, inclusive, [and] secure Israel" within the Democratic Party. The organization’s documented platform is centered on maintaining a robust U.S.-Israel relationship and supporting Israel’s right to exist as a Jewish state.

*   **Official Purpose:** According to its bylaws and mission statement, the club works to "advocate for the values of the Jewish community... including... supporting Israel as a democratic, inclusive, and secure Jewish state."
    *   *Source:* [Jewish Democratic Coalition of the Bay Area – Purpose](https://www.jdcsv.com/purpose)
*   **Two-State Solution:** The JDCSV platform explicitly supports a "two-state solution that allows Palestinian citizens to exercise self-determination within their borders and Israeli citizens to exercise self-determination within their borders."
    *   *Source:* [JDCBA Home – Platform Statement](https://www.jdcsv.com/)
*   **Zionist Framework:** The organization frames its advocacy around the preservation of Israel's character as a Jewish and democratic state, a position it describes as a "core Jewish value." 
    *   *Source:* [JDCBA Endorsements – Pro-Israel Criteria](https://www.jdcsv.com/endorsements)

### 2. Endorsement Criteria and Candidate Vetting
The JDCSV uses a formal vetting process to ensure endorsed candidates align with its pro-Israel platform. Candidates are required to complete questionnaires and participate in interviews that evaluate their commitment to Israel and the U.S.-Israel alliance.

*   **Pro-Israel Requirements:** Endorsements are granted to candidates who "support Israel as a Jewish and democratic state" and demonstrate a "history and background of supporting the Jewish community."
    *   *Source:* [JDCBA – 2024 General Election Endorsements](https://www.jdcsv.com/endorsements)
*   **Case Study (2024 Endorsements):** The club endorsed Adam Schiff for U.S. Senate and several state legislators, including Josh Becker and Scott Wiener, specifically noting their alignment with the club's pro-Israel and pro-Jewish values.
    *   *Source:* [JDCBA – Endorsed Candidates List](https://www.jdcsv.com/endorsements)
*   **Case Study (2023 Senate Forum):** The JDCSV participated in a forum with U.S. Representative Barbara Lee to "clarify her support for Israel." Club President Dorene Kastelman noted the forum was intended to evaluate Lee's positions on Israel, antisemitism, and the BDS movement.
    *   *Source:* [J. Weekly – Rep. Barbara Lee Touts Support of Israel](https://jweekly.com/2023/04/21/rep-barbara-lee-touts-support-of-israel-in-bid-to-replace-feinstein/)

### 3. Policy Advocacy and Counter-BDS Activity
The JDCSV engages in active lobbying within the California Democratic Party to secure pro-Israel language in party platforms and to oppose movements seeking to delegitimize or sanction the state of Israel.

*   **Opposition to BDS:** The club actively "rails against boycotts and sanctions of Israel" and works to ensure party resolutions do not support the Boycott, Divestment, and Sanctions (BDS) movement.
    *   *Source:* [San Jose Inside – County Democratic Party Split on Israeli-Palestinian Conflict](https://www.sanjoseinside.com/news/county-democratic-party-split-on-israeli-palestinian-conflict/)
*   **Platform Lobbying:** JDCSV, as part of the California Jewish Democrats (CJD) network, lobbied to remove "one-sided language" from the 2024 California Democratic Party platform that would have conditioned military aid to Israel or used the term "genocide" to describe Israeli military actions.
    *   *Source:* [California Jewish Democrats – Platform Deal Report](https://www.dfi-ca.org/news/platform-deal-eliminates-proposed-genocide-and-aid-conditioning-language)
*   **Adoption of IHRA Definition:** The club advocates for the use of the International Holocaust Remembrance Alliance (IHRA) Working Definition of Antisemitism, which includes certain types of criticism of Israel as examples of antisemitic rhetoric.
    *   *Source:* [JDCBA – IHRA Definition Statement](https://www.jdcsv.com/)

### 4. Intra-Party Organizational Influence
The JDCSV operates as a regional hub for pro-Israel organizing within the Santa Clara County and San Mateo County Democratic parties, often collaborating with state and national organizations to counter "anti-Israel sentiment" in the progressive wing of the party.

*   **ADEM Elections:** The club mobilized members to run in the Assembly District Election Meetings (ADEMs) to counter "fringe groups" that sought to "change the party's support for Israel to support a unilateral arms embargo."
    *   *Source:* [California Jewish Democrats – 2025 ADEM Election Report](https://www.dfi-ca.org/news/adem-election-results)
*   **State-Level Affiliation:** Through its membership in the California Jewish Democrats, JDCSV coordinates with other regional clubs to maintain a "bridge between the California Democratic Party and the pro-Israel community."
    *   *Source:* [California Jewish Democrats – Who We Are](https://www.dfi-ca.org/who-we-are)
