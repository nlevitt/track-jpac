# Contributor Report: JEANNE D. FISHER

### Overview and Identification
Jeanne D. Fisher (Jeanne Donovan Fisher) is a prominent American philanthropist and political donor based in New York City. She is the widow of **Richard B. Fisher** (1936–2004), the former Chairman of Morgan Stanley. While the provided context associates the name "Jeanne D. Fisher" with the founders of **Gap Inc./Pisces Inc.**, public records indicate that the Gap founders are **Donald G. Fisher** and **Doris F. Fisher**. Jeanne Donovan Fisher is a distinct individual, though she frequently appears on high-level donor rolls alongside the San Francisco-based Fisher family in international arts and Jewish philanthropic contexts [1.7, 1.8].

### Documentation of Zionist Advocacy and Support for Israel
Fisher’s support for Israel and Zionist organizations is primarily documented through her significant contributions to donor-advised funds and direct participation in major pro-Israel foundations.

*   **Jewish Communal Fund (JCF):** Fisher is a documented donor to the **Jewish Communal Fund** [1.9, 1.14]. The JCF is one of the largest donor-advised funds in the United States and has been identified by advocacy monitors as a primary vehicle for funding Zionist organizations, including the **American Israel Public Affairs Committee (AIPAC)**, the **Friends of the Israel Defense Forces (FIDF)**, and **Birthright Israel** [1.12, 1.15]. 
*   **Birthright Israel Foundation:** The Fisher family name is prominently associated with the **Birthright Israel Foundation**, an organization that sponsors free trips to Israel for young Jewish adults with the goal of "strengthening Jewish identity" and "connecting them to their homeland" [1.4, 1.6]. While specific individual grant amounts from Jeanne D. Fisher are often bundled through family foundations or donor-advised funds, she is regularly acknowledged in the foundation's donor networks [1.9].
*   **Koret Foundation and San Francisco Ties:** Fisher is frequently listed in donor circles associated with the **Koret Foundation**, a major pro-Israel grantmaking organization based in San Francisco. The Koret Foundation is a significant funder of the **Koret Israel Economic Development Funds** and various programs aimed at bolstering the Israeli economy and security [1.5, 1.8].
*   **Cultural Zionist Advocacy:** Fisher has held leadership roles in institutions that promote Israeli cultural interests internationally. She has been a supporter of the **Israel Museum** in Jerusalem and is associated with the **British Friends of the Art Museums of Israel (BFAMI)**, which uses art and cultural exchange to build international support for the State of Israel [1.2].

### Political Contributions
Fisher is a frequent donor to political campaigns and committees that maintain strong pro-Israel platforms. Her contributions are often filed from New York addresses (notably zip code 10011) but occasionally appear in lists alongside California-based donors due to her involvement with West Coast-headquartered firms like **Hall Capital Partners** [1.2, 1.3].
