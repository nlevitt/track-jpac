# Contributor Report: Farber, Jeff

Jeffrey A. Farber is the Chief Executive Officer of the **Koret Foundation**, a San Francisco-based private philanthropic organization. Since assuming the role in 2005, Farber has directed the foundation’s strategic funding toward strengthening the U.S.-Israel relationship and supporting Zionist advocacy through high-level institutional grants and leadership in pro-Israel political organizations.

### 1. Leadership in Pro-Israel Political Advocacy
Farber maintains significant leadership roles in the **American Israel Public Affairs Committee (AIPAC)**, a primary pro-Israel lobbying organization in the United States. 
*   **AIPAC National Executive Committee:** Farber serves on the National Executive Committee of AIPAC and is the past chair of AIPAC of Northern California ([Koret Foundation](https://koret.org/israel-at-75-the-wonder-of-a-jewish-state/)).
*   **Onward Israel:** He is a member of the board of directors for **Onward Israel**, an organization that facilitates immersive experiences in Israel for young adults to strengthen their connection to the state ([Shalem College](https://shalem.ac.il/en/leadership-item/jeffrey-farber/)).
*   **Public Statements:** Farber has publicly stated, “I have never felt more committed to ensuring a strong U.S.-Israel relationship,” and describes Israel as the "homeland for the Jewish people" ([Koret Foundation](https://koret.org/israel-at-75-the-wonder-of-a-jewish-state/)).

### 2. Philanthropic Funding for Zionist and Security Initiatives
Under Farber’s leadership, the Koret Foundation has allocated millions of dollars to organizations that promote Zionist education, Israeli military support, and policy advocacy.

*   **Tel Aviv University (TAU):** In 2022, the foundation awarded a **$10 million grant** to establish the **Koret Center for Jewish Civilization** at TAU. The center is intended to "bridge divides" and improve relationships between Israel and the Jewish diaspora ([Jerusalem Post via Koret](https://koret.org/foundation-news-archives/)).
*   **Shalem College:** The foundation provided a **$5 million grant** to Shalem College, Israel’s first liberal arts college, to expand the "Koret Jewish Peoplehood Project," which educates students on the "pro-Israel movement in the U.S." ([Shalem College](https://shalem.ac.il/en/koret-foundation-awards-5-million-grant-to-shalem-college/)).
*   **American Israel Education Foundation (AIEF):** The Koret Foundation is a documented funder of AIEF, the charitable affiliate of AIPAC that conducts educational programs and congressional trips to Israel ([Medium](https://medium.com/@Countertrac/san-franciscos-koret-foundation-funds-pro-settler-and-anti-muslim-hate-groups-cc20e9834241)).
*   **Friends of the Israel Defense Forces (FIDF):** Koret has provided financial support to FIDF, an organization that raises funds for the social and educational needs of Israeli soldiers ([Medium](https://medium.com/@Countertrac/san-franciscos-koret-foundation-funds-pro-settler-and-anti-muslim-hate-groups-cc20e9834241); [eJewishPhilanthropy](https://ejewishphilanthropy.com/war-breaks-out-inside-friends-of-the-idf/)).

### 3. Funding for Policy and Conflict Advocacy Groups
The Koret Foundation has faced scrutiny for its financial support of organizations described by critics and civil rights groups as promoting "anti-Muslim" or "pro-settler" agendas.
*   **Middle East Forum (MEF):** Koret has provided funding to the Middle East Forum, led by Daniel Pipes. MEF is known for its "Campus Watch" project and advocacy for "Israel Victory" over Palestinian national movements ([Foundation Center 990-PF](https://search.foundationcenter.org/990/990_pdf_archive/941/941624987/941624987_201612_990PF.pdf); [Medium](https://medium.com/@Countertrac/san-franciscos-koret-foundation-funds-pro-settler-and-anti-muslim-hate-groups-cc20e9834241)).
*   **Center for Security Policy (CSP):** The foundation has been identified as a donor to the CSP, a think tank that has been criticized by the Southern Poverty Law Center for promoting conspiracy theories regarding Islamic influence in the U.S. ([Medium](https://medium.com/@Countertrac/san-franciscos-koret-foundation-funds-pro-settler-and-anti-muslim-hate-groups-cc20e9834241)).
*   **StandWithUs:** Koret funds StandWithUs, an organization that conducts campus-based Israel advocacy and counters the Boycott, Divestment, and Sanctions (BDS) movement ([Medium](https://medium.com/@Countertrac/san-franciscos-koret-foundation-funds-pro-settler-and-anti-muslim-hate-groups-cc20e9834241)).

### 4. Financial Status
As of 2024, the Koret Foundation reported total assets of approximately **$316 million** and annual expenses of **$40.4 million**, representing a significant source of capital for pro-Israel and Zionist-aligned initiatives ([ProPublica Nonprofit Explorer](https://projects.propublica.org/nonprofits/organizations/941624987)).
