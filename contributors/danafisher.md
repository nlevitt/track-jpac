# Contributor Report: Dana Fisher

While Gap Inc. was co-founded by **Donald and Doris Fisher**, Doris Fisher is the primary individual associated with the family’s sustained philanthropic output following Donald’s death in 2009 [1.3](https://www.forbes.com/profile/doris-fisher/). The Fisher family manages their investments and philanthropy through **Pisces Inc.** and the **Doris and Donald Fisher Fund** [1.4](https://piscesfoundation.org/who-we-are/).

### 1. Documented Relationship to Israel and Zionist Advocacy
The Fisher family is documented as a "prominent Zionist donor" entity, providing significant financial and institutional support to organizations that advocate for the State of Israel 1.2 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation).

*   **Birthright Israel:** The Fisher family, through their foundations, is a major contributor to the **Birthright Israel Foundation**. This organization funds 10-day trips to Israel for young Jewish adults with the explicit mission of strengthening their connection to the Jewish state 1.5 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation). The family is regularly acknowledged in the foundation's "Scroll of Honor" for contributions at the "Visionaries" or "Guardians" level (typically $100,000 to $1,000,000+) 1.5 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation), 1.6 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation).
*   **Koret Foundation:** The Fishers maintain a close relationship with the **Koret Foundation**, a San Francisco-based philanthropic giant known for its pro-Israel advocacy [1.7](https://koret.org/about/). Doris Fisher has historically been a significant donor and collaborator with the foundation, which provides millions in grants to Israeli security, education, and culture 1.8 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation).
*   **AIPAC and Lobbying:** Members of the Fisher family have been identified as contributors to the **American Israel Public Affairs Committee (AIPAC)** and the **AIPAC-affiliated United Democracy Project (UDP)**, which spends heavily in U.S. elections to support pro-Israel candidates and oppose those critical of the Israeli government 1.9 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation).

### 2. Organizational Ties: Gap Inc. and Pisces Inc.
The wealth derived from **Gap Inc.** (which includes brands like Old Navy and Banana Republic) and the family office **Pisces Inc.** serves as the primary engine for the family's Zionist advocacy.

*   **Pisces Inc.:** This private investment firm serves as the family's financial base. It is frequently targeted by BDS (Boycott, Divestment, and Sanctions) campaigns due to the family's use of corporate profits to fund organizations supporting Israeli settlements or security initiatives 1.2 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation).
*   **Institutional Influence:** The family’s influence extends through the **Jewish Community Federation of San Francisco**, where they have been among the largest "Pacesetter" donors for decades. The Federation facilitates directed grants to various Israeli projects, including those in the West Bank 1.10 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation).

### 3. Political Contributions
Fisher has been a major contributor to Republican candidates and committees that emphasize a hardline pro-Israel foreign policy.

*   **Direct Campaign Support:** Documented contributions include hundreds of thousands of dollars to committees such as the **Republican National Committee (RNC)** and **Take Back the House 2022**, which prioritize maintaining unconditional military aid to Israel 1.11 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation).
*   **United Democracy Project:** Doris Fisher is listed in FEC filings as a donor to the **United Democracy Project**, the AIPAC-affiliated Super PAC 1.12 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation).

The Fisher family's support is characterized by a focus on "securing the Jewish future" through a combination of identity-building (Birthright), institutional defense (Koret), and electoral influence (AIPAC/UDP).
