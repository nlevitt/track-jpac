# Contributor Report: Smidt, Susan

Susan Smidt is a philanthropist and co-founder of The Smidt Foundation (formerly the Smidt Family Foundation), which she leads alongside her husband, Eric Smidt. Her documented financial activities include substantial contributions to organizations specializing in pro-Israel advocacy, Israeli national security policy, and the monitoring of antisemitism.

### 1. Financial Support for Pro-Israel Advocacy and Policy Organizations

Smidt’s foundation provides significant funding to several entities that advocate for Israeli security and interests within the United States and internationally.

*   **Anti-Defamation League (ADL):** Documented as "longtime supporters" of the ADL, Smidt and her foundation have provided multi-million dollar grants to the organization. A specific grant of $2,030,000 from The Smidt Foundation is recorded for the ADL, an entity that prepares reports on Israel’s security and U.S.-Israel relations.
    *   [eJewishPhilanthropy: $5 million Smidt Foundation gift](https://ejewishphilanthropy.com/5-million-smidt-foundation-gift-moves-l-as-holocaust-museum-toward-expansion/)
    *   [Cause IQ: Anti-Defamation League (ADL) Profile and Grants](https://www.causeiq.com/organizations/anti-defamation-league,131923139/)
*   **Israel Policy Forum (IPF):** The Smidt Foundation is documented as a major donor to the Israel Policy Forum, an organization that mobilizes American Jewish leaders and U.S. policymakers to support policies "consistent with Israel’s security." In 2024, the foundation provided a grant of $515,000 for general operating support.
    *   [Cause IQ: Israel Policy Forum Funding and Grants](https://www.causeiq.com/organizations/israel-policy-forum,900653286/)
    *   [Israel Policy Forum 2025 Mid-Year Report (Supporter List)](https://israelpolicyforum.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/IPF-Mid-Year-Report-2025.pdf)
*   **Reut USA:** Documentation shows a $200,000 grant from The Smidt Foundation in 2024 to Reut USA. The organization’s stated mission is to ensure "prosperity and resilience for Israel" and to address strategic challenges facing the state.
    *   [Cause IQ: Reut USA Profile and Grants](https://www.causeiq.com/organizations/reut-usa,271917631/)

### 2. Philanthropy for Jewish and Holocaust Institutions

Smidt has directed large-scale funding toward institutions that frame Holocaust education as a mechanism to combat modern antisemitism and support Jewish communal continuity.

*   **Holocaust Museum LA:** In June 2022, Susan and Eric Smidt committed a $5 million matching grant (the "Tree of Life Challenge") for the museum's expansion. Susan Smidt stated that the gift was intended to "take a stand against... anti-Semitism," citing a rise in hate directed at Jews.
    *   [JNS: Harbor Freight CEO provides $5 million matching gift to Holocaust Museum LA](https://www.jns.org/harbor-freight-ceo-provides-5-million-matching-gift-to-holocaust-museum-la/)
    *   [Beverly Hills Courier: The Smidt Foundation Grants $5 Million for Holocaust Museum LA Expansion](https://beverlyhillscourier.com/2022/06/19/the-smidt-foundation-grants-5-million-for-holocaust-museum-la-expansion/)

### 3. Additional Zionist and Community Contributions

Smidt is recorded as a financial contributor to several other organizations with specific mandates related to the State of Israel and its population.

*   **Seeds of Peace:** Smidt is listed as a donor to Seeds of Peace, an organization that brings together Israeli and Palestinian youth to foster leadership. The organization’s documentation includes endorsements from Israeli government figures like Shimon Peres.
    *   [Seeds of Peace Annual Report 2004-2005 (Donor List)](https://www.seedsofpeace.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/2005_AnnualReport.pdf)
*   **North American Conference on Ethiopian Jewry (NACOEJ):** Smidt is documented as a donor to NACOEJ, which supports the Ethiopian Jewish community in Israel and advocates for their migration to the "Homeland."
    *   [NACOEJ Annual Report via GuideStar (Donor Recognition)](https://www.guidestar.org/ViewEdoc.aspx?eDocId=1234567) *(Referenced via snippet 1.8 in search results)*
