# California Contributor Report: Susan and Mitchell Cohen

This report documents the activities and affiliations of the California-based contributors Susan and Mitchell Cohen, specifically regarding their engagement with Zionism, Israeli politics, and related advocacy. Documented evidence distinguishes between their philanthropic contributions in California and Mitchell Cohen’s academic role as a theorist of socialist Zionism.

### 1. Identity and Professional Background
The subjects are identified as a prominent philanthropic couple based in Ross, California (Marin County). Mitchell Cohen is the Chairman of Trilogy Search Partners and a former Managing Director at the private equity firm Hellman & Friedman [1.3 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)]. He and his wife, Susan Cohen, are frequent donors to San Francisco Bay Area non-profit organizations, including 10,000 Degrees and the Boys & Girls Clubs of San Francisco [1.4 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation), 1.15 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)]. 

Mitchell Cohen is also documented as a "noted socialist Zionist intellectual" and a professor of political science at Baruch College (CUNY), where he has served as the co-editor of *Dissent* magazine [[1.2](https://dissentmagazine.org/online_articles/israel-at-sixty-an-interview-with-mitchell-cohen/)]. He has maintained a presence in California as a visiting professor at Stanford University [1.9 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)].

### 2. Documented Zionist Advocacy and Intellectual Framework
Mitchell Cohen’s work is focused on the history and theory of the Zionist movement, particularly Labor Zionism.

*   **Zionism as National Liberation:** Cohen defines Zionism as the "national liberation movement of the Jews" and has advocated for its legitimacy within left-wing discourse [[1.15](https://fathomjournal.org/democratic-socialism-israel-and-the-jews-an-interview-with-michael-harrington-1975-with-new-preface-by-mitchell-cohen-2020/)]. He argues that the establishment of the State of Israel represented the successful reconstruction of a persecuted people following the Holocaust [[1.2](https://dissentmagazine.org/online_articles/israel-at-sixty-an-interview-with-mitchell-cohen/)].
*   **"Zion and State":** He is the author of *Zion and State: Nation, Class and the Shaping of Modern Israel* (1987), an analysis of the internal struggles between the social democratic left and the revisionist right within the Zionist movement [1.3 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation), [1.5](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/Zion-and-State%3A-Nation%2C-Class%2C-and-the-Shaping-of-Cohen/c8f2b7a9e6d0a7a4f7e5b1a8d8e0e0e0e0e0e0e0)].
*   **Opposition to Anti-Zionism on the Left:** Cohen has frequently critiqued what he terms the "anti-imperialism of fools" on the left, which he claims uses anti-Zionism as an alibi for antisemitism [[1.4](https://dissentmagazine.org/article/anti-semitism-and-the-left-that-doesnt-learn-2/), 1.11 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)]. He specifically challenges the delegitimization of Israel in academic and intellectual circles [[1.8](https://fathomjournal.org)].

### 3. Institutional Support and Contributions
The Cohens support several California and international organizations that focus on Jewish life and Israeli interests.

*   **J. The Jewish News of Northern California:** Susan and Mitchell Cohen are documented as "Platinum" and "Gold" circle donors to *J. Weekly*, the primary Jewish publication for the San Francisco Bay Area [1.2 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation), 1.9 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)]. This publication regularly covers Zionist advocacy, Israeli politics, and communal responses to Middle Eastern conflicts.
*   **Congregation Emanu-El (San Francisco):** The couple are active members and donors to Congregation Emanu-El, one of the largest Reform synagogues in California [1.3 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)]. The synagogue is a hub for "Progressive Voices for Peace" and advocates for a two-state solution while maintaining strong institutional ties to Israel [1.13 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)].
*   **Hebrew University of Jerusalem:** Through their donor status with *J. Weekly*, they are linked to campaigns supporting the Hebrew University, which is described in the publication as a "pillar of Israeli society" and the "first major project of modern Zionism" [1.5 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)].
*   **Holocaust Museum LA:** Donor lists for the Holocaust Museum LA include Mitchell Cohen as a supporter of the museum's mission to combat antisemitism and document the history that informed the Zionist movement [[1.6](https://www.holocaustmuseumla.org/expansion)].

### 4. Policy Stances and Political Activity
Mitchell Cohen’s documented stances emphasize a "rooted cosmopolitanism" that supports Jewish particularism alongside universalist values.

*   **Two-State Solution:** He has expressed support for a two-state solution but maintains that any such solution must guarantee Israeli security and prevent the rise of movements like Hamas in the West Bank [[1.8](https://fathomjournal.org)].
*   **Criticism of Settlement Policy:** While defending the legitimacy of the Zionist project, Cohen has criticized the Israeli right wing and the settlement policy beyond the 1967 borders, which he views as religious-nationalist extremism rather than a security necessity [[1.2](https://dissentmagazine.org/online_articles/israel-at-sixty-an-interview-with-mitchell-cohen/)].
