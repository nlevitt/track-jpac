# Contributor Report: California Jewish Legislative Caucus Leadership PAC

The **California Jewish Legislative Caucus Leadership PAC** is a political action committee (PAC) associated with the [California Legislative Jewish Caucus](https://jewishcaucus.legislature.ca.gov/) (CLJC). The PAC was established to generate financial support for candidates described by its founders as "Israel-friendly." It operates in coordination with the [Jewish Public Affairs Committee of California](https://jewishcal.org/) (JPAC), recently rebranded as "Jewish California," which serves as the primary advocacy and lobbying umbrella for the state's Jewish institutional interests.

### 1. Mandate and Zionist Advocacy
The PAC’s primary objective, as stated by its founding chair, Senator Marty Block, is to "raise money to support Israel-friendly candidates" ([Source](https://jewishcaucus.legislature.ca.gov/content/california-legislators-form-jewish-caucus)). The associated Legislative Jewish Caucus is documented as being "dedicated to... countering antisemitism, anti-Zionism and anti-Israel narratives" and "strengthening California–Israel economic, technological and cultural ties" ([Source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/California_Legislative_Jewish_Caucus)).

The organization’s relationship to Zionist advocacy is characterized by its support for legislation and policies that favor the State of Israel:
*   **Anti-BDS Legislation:** The group has spearheaded efforts to oppose the Boycott, Divestment, and Sanctions (BDS) movement, including supporting [SB 1287](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billNavClient.xhtml?bill_id=202320240SB1287), which aims to prevent "harassment" and "intimidation" targeting pro-Israel students on campus ([Source](https://jewishcaucus.legislature.ca.gov/news/2024-05-20-jewish-caucus-announces-2024-priority-legislation)).
*   **Curriculum Intervention:** The PAC and its affiliates have advocated for the removal of "anti-Zionist content" from California’s K-12 Ethnic Studies Model Curriculum (ESMC), successfully pushing for the incorporation of the IHRA definition of antisemitism, which includes certain criticisms of Israel ([Source](https://www.jweekly.com/2024/09/20/state-bills-focusing-on-antisemitism-and-holocaust-education-training-await-newsoms-signature/)).
*   **California-Israel Ties:** The caucus has formally endorsed the California-Israel Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) to expand economic and technological partnerships between the state and Israel ([Source](https://jewishcaucus.legislature.ca.gov/news/2018-05-18-jewish-caucus-statement-israel)).

### 2. Financial Activity and Candidate Support
The PAC receives contributions from corporate and labor entities and redistributes these funds to state-level candidates.
*   **Contributions Received:** Major donors include [Pfizer Inc.](https://pfe-pfizercom-d8-prod.s3.amazonaws.com/2018_Political_Action_Committee_Report.pdf) ($5,000 in 2017-2018 cycle) and various labor organizations such as the [California-Nevada Conference of Operating Engineers](https://cal-access.sos.ca.gov/Campaign/Committees/Detail.aspx?id=1022137&view=received) ([Source](https://cal-access.sos.ca.gov/Campaign/Committees/Detail.aspx?id=1362705)).
*   **Candidate Expenditures:** The PAC has documented expenditures for candidates deemed supportive of its mission. For example, it contributed $13,207 to the campaign of Assemblymember [Freddie Rodriguez](https://ballotpedia.org/Freddie_Rodriguez) during the 2022 cycle ([Source](https://ballotpedia.org/Freddie_Rodriguez)).
*   **Candidate Selection:** The group participates in candidate forums where support for Israel is a central litmus test. In 2024, it co-hosted a gubernatorial forum where candidates were required to pledge commitment to "deepening the state's partnership with Israel" and "fighting efforts to boycott the Jewish state" ([Source](https://jewishinsider.com/2024/02/california-gubernatorial-candidates-israel-bds-tom-steyer-eric-swalwell-antonio-villaraigosa-matt-mahan-steve-hilton/)).

### 3. Institutional Links
The PAC is the political arm of a broader network of pro-Israel advocacy in California:
*   **California Legislative Jewish Caucus (CLJC):** The 19-member bicameral group of state legislators that provides the PAC's leadership and legislative agenda ([Source](https://jewishcaucus.legislature.ca.gov/)).
*   **Jewish California (formerly JPAC):** A 501(c)(4) nonprofit that handles direct lobbying for the same priorities, representing over 30 Jewish community organizations across the state ([Source](https://jewishcal.org/)).
*   **Leadership Foundation:** The [California Jewish Legislative Caucus Leadership Foundation](https://digitaldemocracy.calmatters.org/organizations/CA_Jewish_Legislative_Caucus_Leadership_Foundation) is a related 501(c)(3) entity that handles educational and cultural programming, including sponsoring legislative "study tours" to Israel ([Source](https://calmatters.org/politics/2024/09/california-legislators-travel-2024/)).

### 4. Criticism and Identification
Critics of the PAC, such as [JVP-Action](https://www.laprogressive.com/racism/ca-legislative-jewish-caucus) and the [California Faculty Association](https://www.jweekly.com/2023/10/22/updated-csu-faculty-union-conflates-state-jewish-lobby-with-tobacco-and-oil/), have identified it as a specifically Zionist lobbying entity. The California Faculty Association notably included the PAC’s affiliate (JPAC) alongside AIPAC in a questionnaire for political candidates, questioning their acceptance of funds from groups that "harm working people" through their Middle East policy advocacy ([Source](https://www.jweekly.com/2023/10/22/updated-csu-faculty-union-conflates-state-jewish-lobby-with-tobacco-and-oil/)).
