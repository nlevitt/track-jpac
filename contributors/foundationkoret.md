# California Contributor Report: Koret Foundation

The Koret Foundation is a San Francisco-based private philanthropic organization established in 1979. Since its inception, the foundation has distributed over $1 billion in grants, with a significant portion of its strategic initiatives dedicated to supporting the State of Israel and fostering Zionist advocacy through institutional partnerships and educational programs.

### Foundational Mission & Strategic Priorities
The Koret Foundation identifies the support of Israel as one of its original and primary pillars. Its grantmaking is structured around two core "Jewish Community" initiatives that specifically emphasize the foundation's relationship with the State of Israel:

*   **U.S.-Israel Bridge Building:** This priority is defined by a commitment to ensuring Israel's future as a "strategic ally of the United States." The foundation funds academic collaborations, leadership development, and exchange programs designed to strengthen ties between American and Israeli institutions.
    *   **Source:** Koret Foundation - Our Priorities (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)
*   **Jewish Peoplehood:** The foundation defines Israel as the "national homeland" and a "pillar" of Jewish civilization. Its funding in this area aims to increase Jewish identification and strengthen connections between the Diaspora and the State of Israel.
    *   **Source:** [Koret Foundation - Israel at 75: The Wonder of a Jewish State](https://koret.org/grantees/israel-at-75-the-wonder-of-a-jewish-state/)

### Key Institutional Partnerships & Financial Support
The foundation provides multi-million dollar grants to major Israeli academic and social institutions, often in collaboration with the Israeli government.

*   **Tel Aviv University (TAU):** In 2022, Koret announced a $10 million grant to establish the Koret Center for Jewish Civilization at TAU. This partnership, which includes the Israeli government, aims to improve relationships between Jewish communities worldwide and the State of Israel. 
    *   **Source:** [Koret Foundation - $10 Million Grant to Establish Koret Center at TAU](https://koret.org/insights/10-million-koret-foundation-grant-to-establish-koret-center-for-jewish-civilization-at-tau/)
*   **Shalom Hartman Institute:** In 2019, the foundation awarded $10 million to the Shalom Hartman Institute of North America to expand its leadership and educational programs. The institute, headquartered in Jerusalem, focuses on strengthening Jewish peoplehood and educating community leaders about Israel.
    *   **Source:** Jewish Telegraphic Agency - Shalom Hartman Institute Receives Record Grants (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)
*   **Shalem College:** Koret provided a $5 million signature grant to Shalem College in Jerusalem. The foundation notes that through this program, students develop an understanding of "the pro-Israel movement in the US" and the "deeply committed relationship between Israel and diaspora Jewry."
    *   **Source:** [Koret Foundation - Building Bridges Between Bay Area and Israeli Jews](https://koret.org/insights/building-bridges-between-bay-area-and-israeli-jews/)

### Zionist Advocacy & Campus Engagement
The foundation actively supports organizations that promote Zionist perspectives and train individuals for pro-Israel advocacy, particularly on college campuses.

*   **Israel on Campus Coalition (ICC):** Koret has funded the ICC for over a decade. The ICC is dedicated to "inspiring college students in the U.S. to see Israel as a source of pride" and trains student leaders to become "advocates on campus and in the media" for the State of Israel.
    *   **Source:** Koret Foundation - Israel on Campus Coalition Spotlight (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)
*   **American Israel Education Foundation (AIEF):** The foundation is a documented contributor to the AIEF, which is the 501(c)(3) charitable affiliate of the American Israel Public Affairs Committee (AIPAC). AIEF conducts educational seminars in Israel for U.S. members of Congress and political influencers.
    *   **Source:** [Cause IQ - Koret Foundation Grantee List (AIEF)](https://www.causeiq.com/organizations/koret-foundation,941624987/)
*   **IsraAID:** Koret provided the funding to establish the U.S. office of IsraAID, an Israeli humanitarian organization. The foundation describes IsraAID’s volunteers as "ambassadors of Israel around the globe."
    *   **Source:** [Koret Foundation - IsraAID: Bridge Building with Israel](https://koret.org/grantees/israaid-bridge-building-with-israel-through-global-humanitarian-aid/)

### Leadership & Strategic Connections
The leadership of the Koret Foundation maintains direct ties to prominent pro-Israel advocacy organizations. Jeffrey Farber, who has served as the CEO of the Koret Foundation since 2005, is a former chair of AIPAC of Northern California. Farber has stated that he is "committed to ensuring a strong U.S.-Israel relationship" and views Israel as a country "strong enough to protect and defend a homeland for the Jewish people."

*   **Source:** [Koret Foundation - Leadership Profile: Jeff Farber](https://koret.org/grantees/israel-at-75-the-wonder-of-a-jewish-state/)
