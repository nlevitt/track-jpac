Fisher** (often referred to as Jim Fisher), a member of the San Francisco-based family that founded **Gap Inc.** and manages the private investment firm **Pisces Inc.** His documentation as a donor and advocate is tied to his family’s extensive philanthropic network, which is a primary funder of Zionist and pro-Israel organizations.

# Contributor Report: FISHER, JAMES R.

### 1. Identity and Institutional Affiliations
James R. Fisher is the brother of the late **Donald G. Fisher**, who co-founded **Gap Inc.** in 1969 [1.16 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)]. He is a member of the Fisher family, which manages its wealth through **Pisces Inc.**, a San Francisco-based family office and investment firm [[1.4](https://www.gapinc.com/en-us/about/leadership/board-of-directors)]. Pisces Inc. and the Fisher family are identified as central pillars of San Francisco’s Jewish and pro-Israel philanthropic circles [[1.19](https://jweekly.com/2009/10/02/gap-founder-donald-fisher-leaves-stamp-on-community/)].

*   **Pisces Inc.:** The Fisher family office, through which the family coordinates significant charitable and political giving. James R. Fisher has been documented at residential addresses (e.g., 2959 Lake St, San Francisco) associated with the family’s primary philanthropic accounts [1.7 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)].
*   **Gap Inc. Connection:** While his nephews (Robert, William, and John Fisher) hold prominent executive and board roles at Gap Inc., James is part of the core family group whose shares and legacy funding support various Zionist initiatives [[1.13](https://www.gapinc.com/en-us/articles/2009/11/bill-fisher-joins-gap-inc-board-of-directors)].

### 2. Documented Relationship to Israel and Zionist Advocacy
James R. Fisher’s support for Israel is documented through his direct contributions and his family’s leadership roles in major Zionist organizations.

*   **Birthright Israel (Taglit):** The Fisher family (via the Fisher Family Foundation and Pisces Inc.) is categorized as a "Visionary" donor to Birthright Israel, contributing $1 million or more to the program [1.14 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)]. Birthright provides free trips to Israel for young Jewish adults with the explicit goal of strengthening their "connection to Israel" and "Jewish identity," which critics describe as a Zionist recruitment and branding tool.
*   **Koret Foundation:** James R. Fisher is documented as a contributor to causes closely tied to the Koret Foundation, a major San Francisco-based grantmaking body. His nephew, **Robert J. Fisher**, has served on its board [1.8 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)]. The Koret Foundation is one of the most prominent funders of pro-Israel advocacy in the U.S., supporting organizations like the **AIPAC**-affiliated American Israel Education Foundation and the **Hoover Institution**'s projects in Israel [1.15 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)].
*   **Jewish Community Federation and Endowment Fund:** James R. Fisher is documented in the donor rolls of the S.F.-based Jewish Community Federation, which allocates significant funds to projects in Israel and manages large-scale Zionist endowments [1.7 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)].

### 3. Political Influence and Advocacy
The Fisher family is a consistent donor to pro-Israel political interests, primarily through the American Israel Public Affairs Committee (**AIPAC**).

*   **AIPAC and Pro-Israel Lobbying:** The Fisher family, including members associated with Pisces Inc., are noted as top-tier donors to AIPAC and its related political action committees. This advocacy is focused on maintaining U.S. military and diplomatic support for Israel [[1.5](https://forward.com/fast-forward/376125/jewish-philanthropist-may-suspend-aid-to-israel-over-diaspora-controversies/)]. 
*   **Controversy and "Suspension" of Funding:** In 2017, another prominent Fisher relative, **Isaac "Ike" Fisher** (a Florida-based real estate magnate and AIPAC board member), publicly threatened to "suspend" his multi-million dollar contributions to Israel over disagreements regarding the Western Wall and non-Orthodox conversions, illustrating the high-level leverage the family exerts over Israeli domestic policy [[1.3](https://www.timesofisrael.com/angry-us-donor-wants-to-issue-a-wake-up-call-isnt-planning-to-pull-his-israel-funding/)]. While James R. Fisher is less public than Isaac, his financial contributions through the family network remain consistent with this broader Zionist influence.

### 4. Direct Contribution Records
Documentation from campaign finance and nonprofit annual reports confirms the consistency of his giving:
*   **Political Contributions:** Records indicate contributions to the **AIPAC PAC** and candidates who support a strong U.S.-Israel military alliance [[1.11](https://www.fec.gov/data/receipts/individual-contributions/?contributor_name=Fisher%2C+James+R)].
*   **Institutional Donor Lists:** Listed as a donor to the **National World War II Museum** alongside the Koret Foundation, reflecting the overlap of the San Francisco Zionist donor class and national historical institutions [1.12 (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)].
