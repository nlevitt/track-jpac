# Contributor Report: Bloom, Richard

### Overview and Political Status
Richard Hershel Bloom is a former member of the California State Assembly (2012–2022) representing the 50th District and currently serves as a state judge in the Superior Court of Los Angeles County. During his legislative tenure, Bloom was a prominent member of the California Legislative Jewish Caucus and established a record of advocacy for Israel, particularly through the introduction of state-level anti-boycott legislation. 

### Relationship to Israel and Zionist Advocacy

#### Legislative Leadership on Anti-BDS Policy
Bloom was the primary author of Assembly Bill 2844 (AB 2844), known as the **"California Combating the Boycott, Divestment, and Sanctions (BDS) of Israel Act of 2016."** The legislation was designed to prevent the state from entering into contracts with companies that participate in boycotts against Israel. Bloom described the bill as a tool to ensure the state does not "subsidize discrimination" against a sovereign nation.
*   **Source:** [Jewish Journal - Bloom puts forth California anti-BDS bill](https://jewishjournal.com/news/178121/)
*   **Source:** [The Tower - California State Assembly Unanimously Passes Bill Against Israel Boycotts](http://www.thetower.org/3881-californias-state-assembly-unanimously-passes-bill-against-israel-boycotts/)

#### Organizational Affiliations and Support
Bloom has maintained close ties with several major pro-Israel advocacy organizations. He collaborated with the **Israeli-American Council (IAC)** and its political arm, the Israeli-American Coalition for Action, to secure the passage of AB 2844. He has also been a featured speaker at IAC conferences, where strategy for countering the BDS movement was discussed alongside prominent donors like Sheldon Adelson. 
*   **Source:** [Moment Magazine - Getting Political: The Third Israeli-American Council Conference](https://momentmag.com/getting-political-third-israeli-american-council-conference/)
*   **Source:** [Wikipedia - Anti-BDS Laws (Richard Bloom and IAC Collaboration)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anti-BDS_laws#California)

#### Sponsored Travel to Israel
During his time in the Assembly, Bloom participated in multiple sponsored trips to Israel:
*   **2015 Study Tour:** A trip valued at $3,190 was sponsored by Kenes Exhibitions. Bloom reportedly used $6,412 in campaign contributions to cover additional travel expenses for himself and his wife.
*   **2022 Legislative Delegation:** Bloom was among 15 Democratic lawmakers who traveled to Israel on a trip sponsored by the **California Legislative Jewish Caucus Leadership Foundation**, funded in part by the **Koret Foundation** and the **Jewish Federation of Greater Los Angeles**.
*   **Source:** [Sacramento Bee - Sponsored travel for California lawmakers topped $600,000 in 2015](https://www.sacbee.com/news/politics-government/capitol-alert/article63725832.html)
*   **Source:** [CalMatters - Gabriel shares details on Israel trip](https://calmatters.org/newsletters/whatmatters/2022/07/california-legislators-israel-trip/)

### Political Funding and Endorsements
Bloom has received financial and political backing from prominent Zionist figures and organizations:
*   **Democratic Majority for Israel (DMFI):** Bloom was identified as a politician endorsed by the **Democratic Majority for Israel (DMFI) PAC**, an organization that supports pro-Israel Democrats and often intervenes in primaries to oppose candidates perceived as critical of Israel.
*   **Adam Milstein:** Between 2016 and 2022, Bloom received approximately $7,000 in campaign contributions from Adam Milstein, the chairman of the Israeli-American Council (IAC).
