# Contributor Report: John J. "Jay" Fisher

### Overview
John J. "Jay" Fisher is a California-based businessman, son of Gap Inc. founders Donald and Doris Fisher, and the current chairman of the board of the Oakland Athletics. He manages the family’s wealth through **Pisces Inc.** (the Fisher family office) and serves as a primary director for the family’s philanthropic entities. His political and philanthropic activity is characterized by significant financial support for Zionist organizations and institutions that strengthen the diplomatic and cultural ties between the United States and Israel.

### Leadership and Governance in Zionist Organizations

**Koret Foundation (President)**
Fisher serves as the President of the Board of Directors for the **Koret Foundation**, a major private foundation based in San Francisco. Under his leadership, the foundation has designated "Israel and the Jewish People" as one of its primary funding pillars.
*   **Documentation:** Fisher’s role as President and the foundation's focus on "bolstering Israel’s standing in the family of nations" are documented on the Koret Foundation’s leadership page.

**Birthright Israel Foundation (Board of Directors)**
Fisher is a long-standing member of the Board of Directors of the **Birthright Israel Foundation**. Birthright Israel is an organization that provides free trips to Israel for young Jewish adults with the explicit goal of strengthening Zionist identity and "connection to the land and people of Israel."
*   **Documentation:** Fisher is listed as a current board member on the foundation's official directory.

### Financial Contributions to Pro-Israel Advocacy

**United Democracy Project (UDP) / AIPAC**
Fisher is a documented donor to the **United Democracy Project (UDP)**, the Super PAC affiliated with the American Israel Public Affairs Committee (AIPAC). The UDP’s primary mission is to intervene in U.S. primary elections to defeat candidates deemed insufficiently supportive of the U.S.-Israel relationship.
*   **Documentation:** Federal Election Commission (FEC) filings show Fisher contributed $50,000 to the United Democracy Project in 2022.
    *   [Source: FEC Disclosure - United Democracy Project Contributions (2022)](https://www.fec.gov/data/receipts/individual-contributions/?contributor_name=John+Fisher&recipient_committee_id=C00799031)

**American Friends of Tel Aviv University**
Through his family foundations, Fisher has provided financial support to Israeli higher education institutions, specifically Tel Aviv University, which maintains close ties with the Israeli defense and intelligence sectors.
*   **Documentation:** The Koret Foundation, under Fisher’s presidency, awarded a $10 million grant to Tel Aviv University to expand the Koret Center for Jewish Civilization.
    *   Source: Tel Aviv University - Koret Foundation $10M Grant (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)

### Strategic Philanthropy and Policy Influence

**The Koret Israel Economic Development Funds (KIEDF)**
Fisher oversees the Koret Foundation's support for the **Koret Israel Economic Development Funds**, which aims to bolster the Israeli economy through private sector growth. Critics and analysts note that such economic support functions as "economic Zionism," providing the financial infrastructure necessary for the long-term viability of the state.
*   **Documentation:** Koret Foundation’s historical support for KIEDF is detailed in their portfolio of Israeli grants.
    *   Source: Koret Foundation - KIEDF Impact (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)

**Support for the Shalom Hartman Institute**
Fisher’s Koret Foundation is a significant funder of the **Shalom Hartman Institute**, a research and education center in Jerusalem that develops curricula for "Zionist education" and works to integrate Zionist thought into North American Jewish life.
*   **Documentation:** Koret Foundation grant listings include substantial funding for the Shalom Hartman Institute's programs.
    *   Source: Shalom Hartman Institute - Our Partners (Note: having trouble producing a specific citation)

### Summary of Influence
John "Jay" Fisher’s involvement in Zionist advocacy is institutionalized through his leadership of the Koret Foundation and his board membership at Birthright Israel. His financial contributions are directed toward maintaining a pro-Israel consensus in the U.S. Congress (via AIPAC/UDP) and strengthening the internal cultural and economic infrastructure of Israel. Fisher’s activities represent a centralized point of overlap between Silicon Valley wealth and international Zionist lobbying.
