# Contributor Report: Jesse Gabriel for Attorney General 2026

This report documents the political record, legislative activity, and organizational affiliations of California Assemblymember Jesse Gabriel (D-Encino) regarding his relationship with the State of Israel and Zionist advocacy. Gabriel currently serves as the Chair of the Assembly Budget Committee and Co-Chair of the California Legislative Jewish Caucus.

### 1. 2026 Attorney General Campaign and Fundraising
In late 2025, Jesse Gabriel opened a campaign account to run for California Attorney General in 2026. Within one month of launching the account, Gabriel raised over $1 million, drawing financial support from notable pro-Israel figures and donors, including former Meta executive Sheryl Sandberg and Hollywood producer Marc Platt.

*   **Source:** [Sacramento Bee: Who wants to run for California attorney general if Rob Bonta bows out?](https://www.sacbee.com/news/politics-government/capitol-alert/article297593639.html)
*   **Source:** [Transparency USA: Jesse Gabriel Campaign Filings](https://www.transparencyusa.org/ca/candidate/jesse-gabriel)

### 2. Leadership in the California Legislative Jewish Caucus
Gabriel has served as Co-Chair of the California Legislative Jewish Caucus (CLJC) since 2020. Under his leadership, the caucus has explicitly defined its mission to include "countering antisemitism, anti-Zionism, and anti-Israel narratives," as well as "strengthening California–Israel economic, technological, and cultural ties."

*   **Source:** [California Legislative Jewish Caucus Official Site: Leadership](https://jewishcaucus.legislature.ca.gov/officers)
*   **Source:** [Wikipedia: California Legislative Jewish Caucus Mandate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/California_Legislative_Jewish_Caucus)

### 3. Legislative Support for Israel and Zionist Advocacy
Gabriel has championed numerous legislative efforts focused on Israeli interests and the regulation of discourse surrounding the U.S.-Israel relationship.

*   **AB 715 (Antisemitism in Schools):** Gabriel co-authored and championed this 2024/2025 bill, which established a state Antisemitism Prevention Coordinator and incorporated the International Holocaust Remembrance Alliance (IHRA) working definition of antisemitism. Critics, including civil rights organizations, argued the bill conflates criticism of Israeli government policy with antisemitism and risks censoring Palestinian narratives in public schools.
    *   **Source:** [CalMatters: The fight over California’s controversial antisemitism law](https://calmatters.org/education/k-12-education/2024/10/california-antisemitism-law/)
    *   **Source:** [JNS: New bill would ‘gut’ key protections for Jewish students](https://www.jns.org/new-bill-would-gut-key-protections-in-state-law-for-jewish-students-california-lawmakers-say/)
*   **Anti-BDS Legislation (AB 2844):** As a leader of the Jewish Caucus, Gabriel maintains the caucus's legacy of supporting AB 2844, a law designed to prevent the state from contracting with companies that participate in the Boycott, Divestment, and Sanctions (BDS) movement against Israel.
    *   **Source:** [California Legislative Jewish Caucus: Governor signs Anti-BDS bill](https://jewishcaucus.legislature.ca.gov/news/2016-10-04-governor-signs-anti-bds-bill)
*   **House Resolution 99:** In 2022, Gabriel introduced a resolution to officially recognize and honor the contributions of the Israeli-American community in California, an effort spearheaded in coordination with the Israeli-American Civic Action Network (ICAN).
    *   **Source:** [Israeli-American Civic Action Network: California Officially Honors Israeli-Americans](https://israelusa.org/california-officially-honors-israeli-americans/)

### 4. Direct Engagement and Public Statements
Gabriel maintains active ties with Israeli government and community leaders through travel and public advocacy.

*   **2024 Delegation to Israel:** In February 2024, Gabriel co-led a delegation of California lawmakers to Israel, sponsored by the Jewish Federation of Greater Los Angeles and the Jewish Community Relations Council. The trip’s stated purpose was to "bear witness" to the October 7 attacks and reinforce the California-Israel partnership.
    *   **Source:** [CalMatters: How California legislators got more than $820,000 in travel](https://calmatters.org/politics/capitol/2024/09/california-legislator-travel-2023/)
    *   **Source:** [Official Statement: LA area legislators join trip to Israel](https://sd27.senate.ca.gov/news/2024-02-15-la-area-legislators-join-trip-israel-tensions-over-gaza-war-grow-home)
*   **October 7 Statement:** Following the Hamas-led attacks on October 7, 2023, Gabriel issued a formal statement on behalf of the Jewish Caucus declaring, "We stand with Israel" and affirming "Israel’s right and responsibility to defend its civilians."
    *   **Source:** [California Legislative Jewish Caucus: Statement on Terrorist Attacks Against Israel](https://jewishcaucus.legislature.ca.gov/news/2023-10-07-california-legislative-jewish-caucus-releases-statement-horrific-terrorist-attacks)
*   **Opposition to Ceasefire Resolutions:** Gabriel has publicly criticized local ceasefire resolutions, stating that they often "dredge up anti-Jewish hate" and cause "fear and anxiety" within the Jewish community.
    *   **Source:** [Times of Israel: Pro-Israel lawmakers face disruptions at California Capitol](https://www.timesofisrael.com/jewish-radical-anti-israel-protesters-disrupt-start-of-california-legislative-session/)

### 5. Organizational Affiliations
Prior to and during his time in the Assembly, Gabriel has held leadership roles in organizations dedicated to Zionist advocacy and Jewish community interests.

*   **Jewish Federation of Greater Los Angeles:** Gabriel served as a board member of this organization, which is a primary sponsor of his travel to Israel and a key partner in his legislative agenda.
*   **Democrats for Israel (DFI):** Gabriel received the "highest level of recommendation" and "strong support" from Democrats for Israel Los Angeles for his leadership on "Pro-Israel and Jewish community issues."
