# Contributor Report: Google LLC and its Affiliated Entities (Mark Isakowitz)

### Profile Overview
**Mark Isakowitz** served as Google’s Vice President of Government Affairs and Public Policy for the U.S. and Canada from October 2019 until late 2024. In this capacity, he directed Google’s federal and state-level lobbying, political contributions, and relationship management with government stakeholders. Isakowitz is a longtime political operative in Washington, D.C., having previously served as Chief of Staff to U.S. Senator Rob Portman (R-OH) and as a managing partner of the lobbying firm Fierce, Isakowitz & Blalock. 

**Direct Source:** [AJC Biography of Mark Isakowitz](https://www.ajc.org/mark-isakowitz)

### Documented Relationship to Israel and Zionist Advocacy

#### 1. Leadership in Pro-Israel Organizations
Isakowitz holds a formal leadership role within the **Republican Jewish Coalition (RJC)**, serving as a member of its Board of Directors. The RJC is a primary advocacy organization dedicated to strengthening ties between the Republican Party and the Jewish community, with a stated "basic principle" of supporting a "pro-Israel foreign policy" and the "importance of Israel as a Jewish state."

#### 2. Oversight of Project Nimbus
During Isakowitz’s tenure as Google’s top lobbyist, the company entered into and maintained **Project Nimbus**, a $1.2 billion cloud computing contract with the Israeli government and military. Under this agreement, Google and Amazon provide artificial intelligence and machine learning services to the Israeli Ministry of Defense and other government agencies. Documentation indicates that Google is contractually prohibited from denying service to specific Israeli government entities, including the military, despite internal protests regarding the technology's use in surveillance and data analysis.

#### 3. Handling of Internal Pro-Israel Policy and Dissent
As head of public policy, Isakowitz oversaw Google’s response to internal dissent regarding Project Nimbus. This included the termination of dozens of employees affiliated with the group "No Tech For Apartheid" following sit-in protests at Google offices in 2024. Isakowitz has personally expressed a "deep commitment to fighting antisemitism" and has publicly praised political figures for their "commitment to Israel."

#### 4. Historical Advocacy and Political Ties
Isakowitz’s background includes extensive participation in high-level Israeli policy forums, such as the **Herzliya Conference**, where he has been a featured participant alongside representatives from the American Israel Public Affairs Committee (AIPAC). His career in D.C. is frequently identified with pro-Israel advocacy; he has been described by Jewish community media as a "top lobbyist" who cites a candidate's "commitment to Jewish issues" as a primary motivation for political alignment.
